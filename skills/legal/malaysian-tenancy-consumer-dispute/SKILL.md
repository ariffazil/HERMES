# Malaysian Tenancy & Consumer Disputes

For tenancy, contractor, service, and small-claims disputes in Malaysia.
Covers strategy, communication, and Tribunal Tuntutan Pengguna Malaysia (TTPM).

## Communication Style

When drafting messages for the user to send to landlords/contractors/service providers:

- **Language**: Bahasa Malaysia, casual ("aku/kau" for peers, "saya/tuan" for formal)
- **Format**: WhatsApp-first. Short. Numbered lists.
- **Tone**: Assertive ("nada keras"). No softeners. Direct demands with deadline.
- **Structure**: Who → What's wrong → Deadline → Options → Consequence if ignored
- **Always include**: A specific date deadline, a clear "OR ELSE" ultimatum

## Tenancy Agreements (Malaysia)

### Key Clauses to Look For

| Clause | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Clause 4(a)** — "Peaceable enjoyment" | Landlord must provide tenant quiet possession. Breached if unit uninhabitable from day 1. |
| **Special Conditions** | Often override standard clauses. Read carefully — aircond servicing might be tenant's responsibility (SC #4 in standard agreements). |
| **Inventory list** | If blank, no record of pre-existing condition → tenant advantage in disputes. |

### Defects at Handover vs Routine Maintenance

Critical distinction:
- **Defect from day 1** = Landlord's responsibility (must deliver habitable unit)
- **Routine maintenance during tenancy** = Check Special Conditions (often tenant's responsibility)

If aircond not working from handover → landlord breached covenant. If it broke after 3 months → SC #4 may make it tenant's cost.

### Deposit Recovery Strategy

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Landlord breaches | Tenant can use deposit as last month's rent, then exit |
| Tenant has leverage | Stay in unit, stop paying rent, use deposit to cover |
| Deposit partially paid | Less at risk — tenant can walk away with minimal loss |

### Unlawful Eviction

Under Specific Relief Act 1950, landlord CANNOT:
- Enter unit without permission
- Change locks
- Cut utilities
- Remove tenant's belongings

Without a court order. This is **trespass + unlawful eviction**. Tenant's response:
1. Call police (report trespass)
2. Show tenancy agreement + payment receipts
3. Police can accompany tenant to retrieve belongings
4. File supplementary police report for the record

## TTPM (Tribunal Tuntutan Pengguna Malaysia)

### When It Works

| Good For | Not Good For |
|---|---|
| Clear contract + clear breach | Verbal agreements only |
| Amount ≤ RM 50,000 | Disputes over RM 50,000 |
| Service/consumer matters | Pure landlord-tenant eviction |
| Paper trail exists | No documentary evidence |

### Process

1. File Borang 1 at TTPM office (RM 5 fee)
2. Bring: contract, receipts, WhatsApp screenshots
3. Hearing scheduled within 1-2 months
4. President Tribunal issues Award (legally binding)
5. If respondent no-shows → automatic judgment for claimant

### Enforcement Reality

Winning a TTPM Award ≠ getting paid. To enforce:
- Go to Magistrates Court
- File Saman Pemula (~RM 100-300)
- Court can issue: Writ of Seizure & Sale, Garnishee Order (freeze bank account)
- Time: 3-6 months additional

**For claims under RM 3,000**: File TTPM for pressure/paper trail, but enforcing to collection may not be cost-effective. Use TTPM as a threat, not a guaranteed payout.

### Strategy

- **For landlord disputes**: Stay in unit, stop paying rent, use deposit as leverage. TTPM as backup threat.
- **For contractor disputes**: TTPM works well — clear service-for-payment relationship.
- **Key**: Always have paper trail (WhatsApp screenshots, bank transfer receipts, written notices).

## TNB (Electricity) — Malaysia

- Account numbers: 12 digits
- Cannot check bill without login (myTNB app) or QuickPay portal
- Change of Tenancy (COT) needed when tenant changes — requires landlord cooperation
- Tip: Don't transfer TNB into tenant's name until landlord settles other obligations
