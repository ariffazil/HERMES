# Deposit Leverage Pattern (Malaysian Tenancy)

Proven strategy for when landlord breaches — tenant has paid partial or full deposit but landlord fails to deliver habitable unit.

## The Pattern

1. **Stay** — possession = leverage. Never leave voluntarily while deposit is at stake.
2. **Stop paying** next month's rent — use the paid deposit as de facto rent.
3. **Use the free month** to find alternative accommodation.
4. **Exit** at month-end. Take timestamped photos of unit condition.
5. **Demand** utility deposit refund + other out-of-pocket costs.
6. **Threaten TTPM** — even if enforcement is uncertain, the threat often gets settlement.

## Deposit Variants

| Deposit Status | Strategy | Net Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Full deposit (RM 4,700) | Stay, don't pay rent for 1-2 months, file TTPM for remainder | RM 0-500 + filing fees |
| Half deposit (RM 1,800) | Stay, don't pay 1 month, walk away | RM 100-200 (stamping only) |
| No deposit | Minimal leverage. File TTPM, but settlement less likely. | Full rent paid as loss |

## Key Principle

**Deposit is NOT the landlord's money until tenancy ends properly.** If landlord breached first, tenant has the right to offset deposit against unpaid rent. Document everything — WhatsApp screenshots, bank transfers, contract pages — for TTPM evidence.
