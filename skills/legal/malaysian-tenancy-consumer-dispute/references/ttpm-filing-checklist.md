# TTPM Filing Checklist

## Before Filing

- [ ] Tenancy agreement / contract (salinan)
- [ ] All payment receipts (bank transfer, cash, screenshot)
- [ ] All WhatsApp/text communication with landlord/contractor
- [ ] Photos/videos of defects (with date)
- [ ] Copy of formal notice sent + proof of delivery
- [ ] IC copy (claimant)

## Filing

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **1. Location** | Pejabat TTPM — Level 15, Putra Place, KL (or nearest state branch) |
| **2. Form** | Borang 1 (Form 1) — fill in Malay or English |
| **3. Fee** | RM 5 |
| **4. Supporting docs** | Attach copies of all evidence above |
| **5. Filing hours** | Weekdays, office hours |
| **6. Hearing date** | Usually 4-8 weeks after filing |

## At Hearing

- Represent yourself (no lawyer needed)
- Bring original documents
- Tell your story chronologically:
  1. What was agreed
  2. What you paid
  3. What they failed to do
  4. What notice you gave
  5. What you're claiming
- President Tribunal will ask questions — answer directly

## After Award

| If | Then |
|---|---|
| Respondent pays within 14 days | Case closed |
| Respondent doesn't pay | File at Magistrates Court to enforce |
| Respondent appeals | Award suspended — wait for appeal decision |

## Enforcement (Magistrates Court)

- File Saman Pemula (Originating Summons)
- Cost: ~RM 100-300
- Time: 3-6 months
- Court can issue:
  - Writ of Seizure & Sale (sita harta)
  - Garnishee Order (bekukan akaun bank)
  - Judgment Debtor Summons (hearing untuk dedahkan aset)

## Realistic Assessment

- Claims under RM 3,000: File TTPM for pressure. Enforcement may not be cost-effective.
- Claims RM 3,000-10,000: Worth pursuing through to enforcement if respondent has identifiable assets.
- Claims over RM 10,000: Consider lawyer. TTPM still viable as first step.
