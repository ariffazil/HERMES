# GEOX Competitive Intelligence & Benchmark Design

> **Core insight:** GEOX doesn't compete on workflow breadth. It competes on the intelligence layer — the one that tests whether an earth claim deserves to live.

## Trigger

Any time you:
- Analyze GEOX's position vs. incumbent geoscience platforms
- Design benchmarks to prove GEOX's differentiation
- Compare GEOX against Petronas AI initiatives (TriCipta AI, AI.SEEK, GEB)
- Evaluate competitor AI tools (Tridiagonal.AI, Geoteric, SLB Lumi, etc.)
- Build competitive artifacts for sovereign review

## The Wedge

**One sentence:**
> DS365 tells you where your model lives. GEOX tells you whether your model deserves to live.

**Sharpened:**
> SLB and Halliburton manage subsurface workflows. GEOX tests whether the earth claim is defensible.

## Competitive Map — Three Layers

### Layer 1: Workflow Engines (incumbents own this)
- Halliburton DS365 / DecisionSpace
- SLB Petrel / DELFI
- Landmark OpenWorks

**Their strength:** 30+ years of installed workflows, enterprise procurement, Petrel-converted users, visualization, multi-user interpretation.
**Their weakness:** They let interpretation become reality too easily. No falsifiability gates. No OBS/DER/INT/SPEC separation. No "kill this model" logic.

### Layer 2: AI Application Layer (emerging)
- Petronas TriCipta AI (JDA model — Beicip-Franlab + AFED Digital)
- AI.SEEK (geospatial AI for exploration)
- GEB (Global Exploration Basin Screening)
- Tridiagonal.AI + IBM (surface equipment optimization)
- Geoteric (AI Faults, AI Horizons — seismic interpretation)

**Their strength:** Domain-specific AI, faster screening, pattern recognition.
**Their weakness:** No constitutional governance. No evidence floors. No human veto. No falsifiability. Pattern-finding, not reasoning.

### Layer 3: Earth Reasoning Substrate (GEOX's lane)
- Epistemic labeling (OBS/DER/INT/SPEC)
- Falsifiability gates
- Evidence provenance
- Alternative hypotheses kept alive
- Claim → challenge → verdict pipeline
- Physics-first reasoning
- Constitutional governance (F1-F13)

**GEOX's strength:** The only system that reasons about what patterns mean — and what they don't.
**GEOX's gap:** Not a workflow engine. Not a visualization platform. Not an enterprise replacement.

## Benchmark Design Principles

### What to Benchmark First (priority order)

1. **GEOX-001: Well-Seismic Truth Test** — Can GEOX catch when a horizon pick is not supported by the well-seismic tie? (HIGHEST leverage)
2. Malay Basin prospect screening — with real well tops
3. Deepwater Sabah amplitude-risk audit — with real seismic
4. Mature field bypassed-pay screen — with production history
5. Full basin-to-prospect loop — after tie discipline is proven

### What NOT to Start With

| Candidate | Reason to delay |
|---|---|
| Malay Basin prospect screening | Too broad; becomes narrative fast |
| Deepwater Sabah amplitude-risk audit | Powerful, but needs seismic discipline first |
| Carbonate platform uncertainty audit | Too facies-specific for first proof |
| Mature field bypassed-pay screen | Needs production + petrophysics integration |
| Full basin-to-prospect loop | Too many moving parts before tie discipline is proven |

### Benchmark Anatomy (GEOX-001 template)

Every benchmark must produce:
1. QC-verified ingested files (LAS, tops, checkshot, seismic)
2. Explicit evidence graph with OBS/DER/INT/SPEC labels
3. Synthetic tie / drift result
4. Claim with epistemic separation
5. Active challenge / alternative interpretation
6. Verdict that can say PROCEED, HOLD, or KILL without pretending certainty

## Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Treating GEOX as a Petrel competitor
**What happens:** You try to compete on visualization, data management, multi-user interpretation — and lose on 30 years of installed base.
**Fix:** GEOX competes on the intelligence layer. It reads from incumbent systems, audits interpretations, classifies uncertainty, recommends next evidence. It does NOT pretend to be the master data store.

### Pitfall 2: Overclaiming GEOX's current capabilities
**What happens:** Claiming GEOX can do full basin-to-prospect when the well-tie/seismic integration is still being wired.
**Fix:** Be honest about gaps. The intelligence layer is proven. The execution plumbing is catching up. State what works and what needs wiring.

### Pitfall 3: Confusing vendor AI with intelligence
**What happens:** Treating TriCipta AI, T.AI, or Geoteric as competitors to GEOX's lane.
**Fix:** They are Layer 2 (application AI). GEOX is Layer 3 (reasoning substrate). Different categories. T.AI doesn't touch geoscience. Geoteric doesn't reason about earth claims. TriCipta is pattern-finding, not falsification.

### Pitfall 4: Building benchmarks on synthetic-only data without acknowledgment
**What happens:** A benchmark "proves" GEOX works but uses entirely synthetic inputs — convincing internally but not to external audiences.
**Fix:** Synthetic benchmarks are valid for pipeline proof. But they need a confidence cap (0.70) and explicit acknowledgment. The real proof point is GEOX-001 with real or realistic well data.
