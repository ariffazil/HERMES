# Sarawak Basin — Geological Reference Data
> **Compiled:** 2026-07-11 from published literature + web research
> **Epistemic tag:** OBS/DER (published sources), INT (interpretive)
> **Use for:** Dossiers, screening memos, prospect assessments on Sarawak offshore blocks

## 1. Tectonic Framework

**Basin:** Sarawak Basin, NW Borneo (part of larger NW Borneo fold-thrust system)
**Provinces:** Baram Delta (north), Balingian (central), Tinjar (central), Central Luconia (south — carbonate platform), North Luconia (deepwater)

### Deformation Episodes
1. **Late Cretaceous–Eocene (79.5–36 Ma):** Block faulting, rift basin formation on Rajang Accretionary Prism basement
2. **Late Oligocene–Mid Miocene (30–20.5 Ma):** Wrench movement and related folding
3. **Mid Pliocene–Holocene (4.0–0 Ma):** Uplift and compressional folding; re-activation of growth faults as strike-slip

### Key Unconformities
- **Middle Miocene Unconformity (MMU, ~16 Ma):** Major stratigraphic hiatus across Bunguran Trough and North Luconia. Recognized throughout Sarawak Basin.

### Province Boundaries
- Baram Delta ↔ Central Luconia: Major NW-SE hinge line (basement fault). Sediment thickness decreases dramatically SW across this hinge.
- Baram Delta extends NE through Brunei into southern Sabah.

## 2. Stratigraphic Cycle System (Ho Kiam Fui, 1978)

Eight major sedimentary cycles based on rapid, widespread transgressions:

| Cycle | Age | Environment | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | Eocene | Deep marine | Marls, silts, shales. Rift fill. |
| II | Late Eocene–Oligocene | Deep marine | Continued deep-water sedimentation |
| III | Oligocene–Early Miocene | Deep marine → transitional | Possible carbonate buildups at platform margins (Luconia) |
| IV | Early–Middle Miocene | Transitional → shallow | Cycle III–IV carbonates proven reservoirs in Central Luconia (porosity up to 20%, pinnacle and platform types) |
| V | Middle–Late Miocene | Shallow marine → deltaic | Major transition. Primary reservoir cycle for Baram Delta fields (including SK 309/311) |
| VI | Late Miocene–Early Pliocene | Deltaic (major growth fault regime) | Baronia field analogue. Dokong-1 gas discovery in Baram Province (Cycle VI–VII). |
| VII | Late Pliocene | Deltaic | Prograding across new growth faults |
| VIII | Quaternary | Deltaic | Youngest sediments |

### Cycle Boundary Criteria (Shell convention)
1. Base = regional transgression (marine shale over fluvial/coastal sands)
2. Sequence = regressive (marine at bottom → coastal plain at top; coarsening-upward)
3. Transgressions are rapid → good time markers within Baram Delta area

## 3. Baram Delta Province — Detailed (Ruks, 1981)

### Structural Style
- **Growth faults:** Arcuate, generally hading north. Result of density contrasts + gravity sliding during deltaic sedimentation. Coincide with ancient coastlines.
- **Compressional folds:** NE-SW trending axes, Late Miocene (Cycle VI). Overprint earlier growth faults.
- **All fields located at intersection of growth faults + anticlinal trends.**
- Resembles Niger Delta and US Gulf Coast but smaller areal extent.

### Sedimentation Model
- Rd/Rs > 1: Regressive (prograding delta)
- Rd/Rs ≈ 1: Equilibrium (stacking vertically)
- Rd/Rs < 1: Transgressive (marine shales transgress shoreward)

### Reservoir Environments (from well log shapes)
- **Funnel (coarsening-up):** Delta fringe environment
- **Cylinder:** Channel environment (constant grain size)
- **Bell (fining-up):** Meander river environment

### Source Rocks
- All hydrocarbons are **landplant-derived** (no marine source rocks detected)
- Best source rock concentration: **coastal plain environment**
- Organic content decreases rapidly toward marine environments
- Source rock recognition difficult — organic material concentrated in very thin layers
- **Generation temperatures:** Oil ~118°C (245°F), Gas ~149°C (300°F)
- **Generation depth:** 3,048–4,115 m (10,000–13,500 ft) — low geothermal gradient ~0.9°C/30m
- **Migration:** Lateral from synclinal kitchen areas up flanks into structures. Vertical re-migration along compressional faults for shallow accumulations.

### Proven Reservoir Properties (general Baram Delta)
- Moderate to poor porosity-permeability (declining with depth)
- Deltaic sandstones: fluvial, coastal plain, paralic, shallow marine

## 4. Central Luconia Province — Carbonate Play

### Limestone Growth Events (Frontiers, 2021)
1. **Event 1 (Upper Eocene–Lower Oligocene):** Oldest limestone growth on elevated topography from Proto South China Sea (PSCS) subduction. Correlates to onshore Engkabang-Karap limestone.
2. **Event 2 (Lower Miocene):** Southern Luconia compressed while northern Luconia subsided during South China Sea rifting.
3. **Event 3 (Middle–Upper Miocene):** Tectonic quiescence enabled **widespread carbonate platform growth** across Luconia.

### Carbonate Buildup Types
- **Pinnacle builds:** Fair to good porosity, decreasing dolomite with depth
- **Platform builds:** Poor to fair porosity (higher mud content)
- **Reservoir:** Cycles III and IV carbonates

### Significance for Baram Delta Adjacent Blocks
The hinge line between Baram Delta and Central Luconia is a **transition, not a wall**. Blocks at the eastern margin of Luconia (like SK 309/311) may have pre-deltaic carbonate buildups beneath the Cycle V–VI clastic overburden.

## 5. Baram Province — Recent Discoveries (2021–2026)

### Dokong-1 (Block SK417, Feb 2021)
- 80m+ gas column in Cycle VI–VII reservoirs
- Sweet gas (contaminant-free)
- Proved Baram Province shallow clastics play still viable
- ~90 km offshore Sarawak, TD 3,810m

### Nangka-1 (Block SK417, Nov 2021)
- Second discovery in SK417
- Sweet gas in Cycle VI clastic reservoirs

### Lang Lebah-2 (Block SK410B, Feb 2021)
- Massive gas discovery, appraisal well
- Largest gas discovery by PTTEP in Malaysia

### Kulintang-1 (Block SK438, 2021)
- Gas discovery, first exploration well in SK438

### Sirung-1 (Block SK405B, Jan 2021)
- Oil + gas column >100m in clastic reservoirs
- TD 2,538m

### Kenyalang Cluster (Blocks SK301B + SK313, 2023–2025)
- PETRONAS Carigali drilled Sinsing-1, Tadom-1 (SK313) and Machinchang-1, Pangkin-1 (SK301B)
- Combined >4 Tcf gas
- TotalEnergies acquired 50% operated stake from PETRONAS (June 2025)
- First production targeted ~2030
