# Hall / Balaguru / Gilligan / Krebs — Condensed Reference Cards

Last verified: 2026-07-03 (Kinabalu Two-Oceanics audit).

## Hall (2013) — canonical rollback paper
- **Citation:** Hall, R. (2013). Contraction and extension in northern Borneo driven by subduction rollback. *J. Asian Earth Sci.* 76, 399–411.
- **Core claim:** Proto-South China Sea subducted southward beneath Borneo (40–15 Ma). At ~15 Ma, Dangerous Grounds continental margin arrived → subduction stopped. New subduction began on Celebes Sea plate, which is rolling back → extension in northern Borneo.
- **Key evidence:** Seismic stratigraphy, tomography, regional plate reconstructions.
- **Use in artifact:** THE canonical citation for two-step model. Cite for Proto-SCS consumption timing, Dangerous Grounds collision, Celebes rollback, extension direction.

## Hall (2012) — SE Asia reconstructions
- **Citation:** Hall, R. (2012). Late Jurassic–Cenozoic reconstructions of the Indonesian region and the Indian Ocean. *Tectonophysics* 570–571, 1–41.
- **Core claim:** Comprehensive SE Asia plate reconstruction from Late Jurassic to present.
- **Use in artifact:** Backstop citation for any SE Asia tectonic timing. Cite when you need to anchor a date.

## Balaguru & Hall (2009) — Sabah tectono-sedimentary synthesis
- **Citation:** Balaguru, A., & Hall, R. (2009). Tectonic Evolution and Sedimentation of Sabah, North Borneo, Malaysia. *AAPG* Search and Discovery Article #30084.
- **Core claim:** Sabah tectono-sedimentary evolution tied to Proto-SCS subduction and Dangerous Grounds collision.
- **Use in artifact:** Sabah-specific AAPG citation. Stronger provenance than Hall 2013 alone for Sabah claims.

## Gilligan et al. (2026) — nBOSS seismic
- **Citation:** Gilligan, A., et al. (2026). Evidence of Subduction, Collision, and Extension in Northern Borneo from the nBOSS seismic experiment. *J. Geophys. Res. Solid Earth* (in review).
- **Core claim:** Wide-angle seismic refraction (nBOSS) resolves Moho at 24 km beneath Kinabalu — **thinned by rollback extension**.
- **Use in artifact:** The killer evidence for crustal thinning prediction. Cite specifically for the 24 km Moho beneath Kinabalu.

## Krebs (2011) — PETRONAS chronosequence
- **Citation:** Krebs, W. N. (2011). PETRONAS chronosequence stratigraphy of Sabah. (Internal PETRONAS biostratigraphic report; cited via GEOX Sabah stratigraphic ontology).
- **Core claim:** Biostratigraphic chronosequence shows no continental metamorphism in Sabah basement.
- **Use in artifact:** THE killer evidence against continental subduction. Cite as "Krebs 2011" via the GEOX Sabah ontology citation chain.

## Franke et al. (2008) — Sabah collision zone seismic
- **Citation:** Franke, D., Barckhausen, U., Heyde, I., Tingay, M., & Ramli, N. (2008). Seismic images of a collision zone offshore NW Sabah/Borneo. *Mar. Petrol. Geol.* 25, 606–624.
- **Core claim:** Seismic reflection images the deep Sabah collision zone; KT-8 detachment visible.
- **Use in artifact:** Anchor for any seismic-velocity or reflection-coefficient argument. The +0.148 RC at 12–21 km depth comes from this paper.

## Cottam et al. (2013) — exhumation
- **Citation:** Cottam, M., Hall, R., Sperber, C., & Armstrong, R. (2013). Neogene rock uplift and erosion in northern Borneo. *J. Geol. Soc. London* 170, 805–816.
- **Core claim:** Apatite fission track + (U-Th)/He records rapid exhumation at Kinabalu since 8 Ma.
- **Use in artifact:** Cite for the >7 mm/yr exhumation rate and the 8 Ma onset.

## Sidek & Hamzah (2018) — NW Sabah structural
- **Citation:** Sidek, M. H. M., & Hamzah, U. (2018). Structural Analysis of NW Sabah Basin. *J. Geol. Eng. Env. Tech.* 3(2).
- **Use in artifact:** Add to references list for completeness; cite for fold-thrust belt geometry in offshore Sabah.

## Tongkul (1994) — Northern Sabah geology
- **Citation:** Tongkul, F. (1994). The geology of Northern Sabah, Malaysia: its relationship to the South China Sea basin. *Tectonophysics* 235, 131–147.
- **Use in artifact:** Early canonical reference. Cite for the South China Sea basin–Sabah relationship framing.

## Rangin et al. (1990) — arc-continent collision
- **Citation:** Rangin, C., et al. (1990). Neogene arc-continent collision in Sabah, N. Borneo. *Bull. Soc. géol. France* 8 VI, 967–989.
- **Use in artifact:** Classic citation for the arc-collision framing. Cite when discussing Proto-SCS arc.

## Taylor & Hayes (1983) — South China Sea origin
- **Citation:** Taylor, B., & Hayes, D. E. (1983). Origin and history of the South China Sea basin. In: Hayes, D.E. (Ed.), *The Tectonic and Geologic Evolution of Southeast Asian Seas and Islands, Part 2*. AGU Geophys. Monogr. 27, 23–56.
- **Use in artifact:** Backstop citation for the South China Sea origin and spreading history.
