# Multi-Perspective Human Reality Mapping

> When Arif needs strategic clarity about multi-person institutional dynamics.
> Ground EVERY claim in direct evidence — quotes, not inference.

## When to Use
- Arif is preparing for a high-stakes meeting with multiple people
- Different people in the same room hold different "realities" about Arif
- He asks "what does X really think about me?" or "how should I handle Y?"

## Method

### 1. Gather All Private Comms
Read every direct message/chat between Arif and each stakeholder. No shortcuts. Cross-platform: WhatsApp, Teams, email — whatever exists on the machine.

### 2. Cross-Reference Timelines
Map when relationships shifted. Look for: deleted messages, disappearing timers, sudden formality, first use of CC, first reprimand.

### 3. Map Each Person's Reality Separately
For each person, answer: what do they SEE, what do they NOT SEE, what do they NEED from Arif, what is their PARADOX (contradictory truths held simultaneously).

### 4. Identify the Hidden Emotional Layer
Most institutional conflict has personal roots. Look for: self-hatred projected outward, extraction masked as care, love bounded by structural constraints.

### 5. Produce Per-Person Strategy
NEVER lump all stakeholders together. Each person gets their own approach. One table, separate columns.

### 6. Ground in Evidence
Every claim about what someone "really thinks" must have a direct quote. No speculation without a message to back it.

## Output Format
```
| Person | Their reality | What they miss | Paradox | Your move |
```
Plus: 3 "DO" and 3 "DON'T" for the meeting as a whole.

## Pitfalls
- Don't turn this into a dossier. This is for Arif's clarity, not institutional record.
- Don't present all analysis unprompted. Wait for him to engage.
- Never diagnose or label humans. Describe patterns. Let Arif name them.
- Compression matters. If you need more than 5 sentences per person, you're overthinking.

## Signature Pattern
Arif's WhatsApp style with colleagues: Penang BM, direct, emotionally open, shares knowledge freely, self-deprecating humor. When messages become formal/deleted/disappearing — that's the signal. The shift IS the data.
