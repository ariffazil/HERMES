## Category 1: Sexual Abuse by Religious Figures

- Quran teacher (tahfiz school, Perak, 2024): charged with 21 counts sexual assault against boys aged 7-11. No background checks, no monitoring.
- Multiple tahfiz school cases (2019-2025): SUHAKAM and SIS documented pattern of physical/sexual abuse by ustaz at boarding schools.
- 2017 Tahfiz Darul Quran Ittifaqiyah fire: 23 children died. School unlicensed, no fire safety, barred windows.
- Pondok schools (traditional Islamic boarding): cases of physical abuse, forced fasting, child deaths. 2023: 7-year-old boy died at pondok in Kedah. 2024: another case in Kelantan.
- Imam/mosque officials: scattered cases — imam charged with molesting children during Quran classes, mosque officials caught in khalwat raids.

## Category 2: Institutional Failures (JAKIM, JAIS, State Religious Depts)

- JAKIM: RM1+ billion annual budget. No public audit. No parliamentary select committee oversight. "Muhasabah" (conversion therapy) programs criticized.
- JAIS: aggressive khalwat raids, entering private homes, hotel rooms. 2024: raided concert, detained couples. Moral policing without evidence.
- Enforcement without accountability: religious enforcement officers entering private spaces, no body cameras, no SOP. Officer's word taken as truth.
- Conversion disputes: one spouse converts children without other's consent. Indira Gandhi case (2009-2018) — husband converted 3 children, Federal Court ruled in mother's favor, enforcement took years.

## Category 3: Financial Misconduct

- Tabung masjid (mosque funds): treasurers stealing donations, imams using funds personally. No mandatory external audit.
- Zakat management: billions collected annually. Distribution opaque. Questions about percentage reaching asnaf. Some states publish reports, many don't.
- Tabung Haji (2018 scandal): RM10 billion in losses hidden through creative accounting. Property deals at inflated prices. CEO/chairman linked to political appointments. Government bailout.

## Category 4: Power Without Oversight

- Structural problem: religious authorities report to state sultans, not elected representatives. No parliamentary select committee. No FOI access. Mufti appointed, not elected.
- Fatwa process: legally binding on Muslims. No public consultation required. No appeal mechanism (except to sultan).
- Moral policing vs real crime: enforcement focuses on khalwat, Ramadan eating, LGBT. Meanwhile child abuse at tahfiz, financial fraud at zakat, corruption at Tabung Haji go under-prosecuted.
