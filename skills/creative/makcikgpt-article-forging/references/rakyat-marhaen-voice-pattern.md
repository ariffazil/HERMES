# Rakyat Marhaen Voice Pattern — MakcikGPT

> How to write for makcik kat pasar, abang grab, pakcik kedai kopi.
> Proven 2026-07-13: PETRONAS ATM article (3 iterations), siasatan-harakah rewrite.

## The Core Rule

**If makcik kampung tak faham tanpa tanya sesiapa — rewrite.**

## What Changes (vs Greek-symbol mode)

| Greek-symbol mode | Rakyat marhaen mode |
|---|---|
| "Δ GROUND" | "Cerita pertama:" |
| "Ω MIND" | "Yang Makcik nampak" |
| "Ξ CAPITAL" | "Apa makna untuk duit hang" |
| "Ψ SOVEREIGN" | "Soalan Makcik" |
| "Epistemic: OBS/INT/SPEC/SHADOW" | (removed entirely) |
| "Nisbah bayar: 71%" | "Hampir separuh" |
| "Vakum akauntabiliti" | "Tak ada siapa periksa" |
| "ROACE jatuh tiga tahun" | "Duit turun tiga tahun berturut-turut" |
| "Segmen Corporate & Others" | "Satu bahagian syarikat" |
| "RM67.6 bilion" | "RM67 bilion — tu lebih kurang satu per lima dari semua duit kerajaan" |

## Number Rules

- Small numbers: write as words ("lima puluh bilion", "tiga puluh dua bilion")
- Big numbers: use RM XX bilion with context ("RM67 bilion — hampir satu per lima dari pendapatan kerajaan")
- Percentages: translate to human language ("hampir separuh" not "49%")
- Ratios: translate to analogy ("macam hang kata gaji saya tiga ribu padahal ada bonus lapan ribu")

## Metaphor Rules

Metaphors MUST be from everyday life. Examples that work:
- "Lembu tu makin gemuk. Bukan makin kurus." (cash pile growing)
- "Macam hang marah kedai runcit sebab harga gula naik, padahal yang untung kilang gula." (blame misdirection)
- "Anak hang ada kawan bernama Gentari. Kawan tu datang rumah. Makan free. Tidur free." (hidden cost)
- "Dua lubang, satu perahu." (simultaneous external + internal drain)

Metaphors that DON'T work:
- "ATM" (too abstract for some)
- "DNA" (too scientific)
- "Ecosystem" (too corporate)

## The Rasa Test

After writing, ask: "Adakah pembaca boleh rasa sakit di belakang nombor ni?"

- Data + manusia = rasa ✅
- Data sahaja = Excel ❌
- Manusia sahaja = emosi tanpa ground ❌

Example: "RM67 bilion sumbangan PETRONAS" = data. "Hang tahu tak berapa harga ikan kat pasar sekarang? RM18 sekilo. Dulu RM10. Dan PETRONAS bagi kerajaan RM67 bilion tahun lepas. Duit tu pergi mana?" = rasa.

## The G Moment (Generational Horizon)

Every article MUST have at least one G moment: a question that forces the reader to confront consequences for the next generation. Arif: "TAMPAR G. Itulah pukulan kebenaran MakcikGPT."

G examples:
- "Anak cucu tinggal apa?" (what's left for our children?)
- "Kalau gas Sarawak masuk syarikat London — anak cucu Sarawak dapat apa?"
- "Duit yang habis hari ni — siapa bayar balik?"

G cannot be answered with data alone — it requires niat (intention). That's what makes it the truth slap.

## Iteration Pattern

Articles may need 2-3 iterations:
1. **Draft** — correct voice, correct data
2. **Audit** — fix data errors caught by external verification
3. **Rewrite** — adjust narrative to match corrected data, add rasa

Proven 2026-07-13: PETRONAS ATM article went through exactly this pattern.

## Detection

Rakyat marhaen mode: "rakyat marhaen", "makcik kampung", "relatable", "jangan susah"
Greek-symbol mode: "kutuk", "hentam", "sharp critique", "for AI lab people"

## Pitfalls

- Don't use Greek symbols in rakyat marhaen mode
- Don't use epistemic labels in body text
- Don't use corporate jargon even in BM
- Don't assume reader knows what "segmen" or "nisbah" means
- Don't end with conclusions — end with questions
- Don't invent human faces — use archetypal but grounded examples
- If makcik kena Google perkataan — rewrite
