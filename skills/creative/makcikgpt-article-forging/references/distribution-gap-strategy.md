# Distribution Gap Strategy — MakcikGPT

> Content is real. Distribution is zero. The gap is pipes, not quality.

## Problem

14 MakcikGPT articles published on arif-fazil.com (as of 2026-07-18). Well-researched, data-driven, Bahasa Makcik, genuinely investigative. But the site gets mostly bot traffic and agent visits. The jiran-jiran who would benefit most are on WhatsApp and Telegram, not browsing personal domains.

"No Medium gate" skips the editorial gate — correct stance. But it also skips the distribution gate. Both gates need replacement.

## Where Jiran-Jiran Actually Are

| Platform | Audience | Format | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **WhatsApp** | Family groups, kampung networks, office groups | Plain text, 3-4 paragraphs, no markdown | Pasteable excerpt |
| **Telegram** | Interest groups, news channels, civic channels | Markdown + link | Channel post with teaser |
| **X/Twitter** | English-speaking professionals, journalists | One sharp question + link | Standalone post |
| **Facebook** | Older demographic, kampung community groups | BM text + link | Shareable post |

## Minimum Viable Distribution (Per Article)

### 1. Telegram Channel Post
- Create or use existing @MakcikGPT channel
- Format: 1 paragraph teaser + article title + link
- Auto-forward from site RSS if possible

### 2. WhatsApp Excerpt
- Extract 3-4 core paragraphs from the article
- Strip all markdown, links, formatting
- Make it pasteable — someone can forward it in a group and it reads naturally
- End with: "Baca penuh: arif-fazil.com/makcikgpt/[slug]"

### 3. Social Teaser
- One sharp question from the article as a standalone post
- Example: "PETRONAS memang ATM. Tapi siapa jaga mesin tu?" + link
- Don't summarize — provoke curiosity

## What NOT To Do

- ❌ Don't auto-spam every platform for every article. Quality over quantity.
- ❌ Don't write "check out my new article" posts. Write the SHARP QUESTION that makes people click.
- ❌ Don't use English teasers for BM articles. Match the language.
- ❌ Don't create a newsletter. Jiran-jiran don't subscribe to newsletters. They share in groups.
- ❌ Don't measure success by pageviews. Measure by forwards — if someone pasted it in a WhatsApp group, it worked.

## Anti-Pattern: "Published = Done"

The trap: agent deploys article → moves to next task → article sits unseen.

The fix: deploy is Stage 6. Distribution is Stage 8. They are different stages with different deliverables. Deploy = the article exists on the web. Distribution = the article reaches humans.

## Evidence

Session 2026-07-18: Strategic review of arif-fazil.com revealed:
- 14 MakcikGPT articles, most recent July 14 (4 days prior)
- Content quality: high — real data, real calls, genuine investigation
- Distribution: zero — no Telegram channel, no WhatsApp format, no social forwarding
- The three AVO papers: same problem (published, not distributed)
- Human essays: alive and current (most recent June 7), but recency not obvious to visitors

Key quote: "Civic journalism that nobody reads is just a blog with better framing."
