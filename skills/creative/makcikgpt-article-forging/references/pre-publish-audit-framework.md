# MakcikGPT Pre-Publish Audit Framework — T×A×M×P×G×R

> **Proven:** 2026-07-13. PETRONAS ATM article had RM55bn (should be RM101.6bn). Gemini external audit caught it. Internal review missed it. This framework exists because self-review alone is insufficient.

---

## The 6 Factors

| Factor | Name | Question | Fail consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **T** | Truth fidelity | Every number verified against primary source? Year attribution correct? Fiscal year cadence respected? | Propaganda |
| **A** | Cognitive access | Makcik kampung boleh faham tanpa tanya sesiapa? Zero jargon? Numbers framed as human impact? | Laporan elit |
| **M** | Moral meaning | "Apa nombor ni buat kepada kehidupan manusia?" Connected to amanah, maruah, anak cucu? | Data kosong |
| **P** | Power legibility | Siapa untung? Siapa rugi? Siapa ambil? Struktur kuasa terdedah? | Komunikasi korporat |
| **G** | Generational horizon | "Anak cucu tinggal apa?" Long-term consequence surfaced? | Short-termism |
| **R** | Correction/memory | Uncertainty acknowledged? Can be corrected later? Sources cited? | Mitos beku |

**G is the zero-test.** Arif: "TAMPAR G. Itulah pukulan kebenaran MakcikGPT." Every article MUST have at least one G moment. Without G, MakcikGPT is just a translator. With G, she's a guardian of the future.

---

## Zero-Factor Rule

Kalau mana-mana factor = 0, article TAK BOLEH publish. Fix dulu.

---

## Rasa Audit (post-T×A×M×P×G×R)

After passing all 6 factors, run one final check: does the article have **RASA**?

**Rasa = embodied feeling.** Data + manusia = rasa. Data sahaja = Excel. Manusia sahaja = emosi tanpa ground.

Test: "Adakah pembaca boleh rasa sakit di belakang nombor ni?"

If the article has correct numbers but no human face — it fails rasa. Fix: add a specific person (jiran, anak buah, engineer, makcik) who bears the consequence. The human face cannot be invented — it must be archetypal but grounded.

Examples from session 2026-07-13:
- **Before (no rasa):** "Kerajaan ambil separuh. Gentari bakar dalam senyap."
- **After (rasa):** "Makcik ada jiran — kerja PETRONAS 22 tahun. Bulan lepas kena MSS. Dia kata: 'Anak saya baru masuk universiti.'"

Rasa is what separates MakcikGPT from a financial analyst in BM clothing.

---

## Audit Process

### Step 1: Self-audit (author)
Go through each factor. Score honestly. If any factor < 0.5, fix before proceeding.

### Step 2: Number extraction
Pull every specific number from the article:
- Financial figures (RM, USD, percentages)
- Dates (years, quarters, specific dates)
- Names (people, companies, positions)
- Claims (quotes, attributions)

### Step 3: Source verification
For each number, verify against primary source:
- PETRONAS: IR2025 five-year table (page 218), FY2025 IFR, segment disclosures
- Government: Budget documents, Finance Ministry statements, PM speeches
- Market data: TradingEconomics, EIA, Reuters, Bloomberg
- Court/legal: NST, Borneo Post, court filing databases

**Critical pitfall:** PETRONAS Group reports HALF-YEARLY, not quarterly. "Q1 2026 PETRONAS Group" does not exist. Listed subsidiaries (PGB, PCG, PETDAG) report quarterly.

### Step 4: External verification
Run at least ONE external check (different model, different source) on the highest-risk number. In 2026-07-13:
- Hermes internal review: missed RM55bn error
- Gemini external audit: caught RM55bn should be RM101.6bn
- Lesson: self-review alone is insufficient

### Step 5: Voice check
Read the article aloud (mentally). Does it sound like a makcik talking to her neighbor? Or does it sound like a report?

Red flags:
- "Epistemic:" anywhere in the text → strip
- "Δ" / "Ω" / "Ξ" / "Ψ" section headers → replace with "Cerita pertama:" etc.
- "Payout ratio" / "ROACE" / "segment" → translate to human language
- "RM67.6 bilion" without context → frame as "satu per lima dari semua duit kerajaan"
- English paragraphs in body → translate or remove

### Step 6: Publish
Only after Steps 1-5 pass.

## Known Pitfalls (from session 2026-07-13)

1. **Fiscal year mixing:** Don't use FY2024 numbers and call them FY2022. Always check the five-year table in IR page 218.

2. **PETRONAS Group vs listed subsidiaries:** PETRONAS Group reports half-yearly. PGB/PCG/PETDAG report quarterly. Don't mix.

3. **"Government milks PETRONAS" narrative:** FY2022 PAT was RM101.6bn (record), dividend RM50bn (49% payout). This SUPPORTS the extraction narrative. Don't cherry-pick years.

4. **Gentari never disclosed:** Gentari standalone PAT is NEVER published. Inferred from Corp & Others segment (~RM -1.0 to -1.25bn/year). Always label as INT, not OBS.

5. **Historical figure attributions:** Verify every quote against the SOURCE ARTICLE, not search engine. In 2026-07-13, initial verification failed for "Rastam fight like bulldogs" because searching "PETRONAS CEO Rastam" found RHB Bank's Rastam Shahrom. The real Rastam Hadi was a founding executive director (deputy governor Bank Negara), NOT a CEO. The quote was verified via The Edge Malaysia article "My Say: The Petronas DNA" (Feb 2010) by Azam Aris. Lesson: search for the article/source containing the quote, not the person's title.

6. **Subsidy vs dividend framing:** PM says "5-6%" (dividend only). Total contribution is 19.7% (dividend + tax + payments). Both are technically correct — always show both.

7. **Big numbers need human framing:** "RM67.6 bilion" = meaningless to makcik kampung. Frame as "satu per lima dari semua duit kerajaan" or "hampir dua kali ganda dari dividen PETRONAS."

8. **Rasa comes LAST, not first.** Data accuracy (T factor) must pass BEFORE adding human stories. Don't add a compelling human face to wrong numbers — that makes propaganda, not journalism.

## Formula (Conceptual, Not Computational)

C_Makcik = T × A × M × P × G × R

If any factor = 0, product = 0. Article fails.

This is a thinking framework, not a calculation. Use it to structure review, not to compute scores.
