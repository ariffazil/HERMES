# Narrative Debunking Pattern — MakcikGPT

> How to write articles that debunk popular narratives without losing the reader.
> Proven 2026-07-13: PETRONAS ATM article (3 iterations, Gemini audit PASSED F2).

## The Pattern: "Accept Then Attack"

When debunking a popular narrative (e.g., "government milks PETRONAS"), the MOST effective pattern is NOT to deny the narrative. Instead:

1. **Accept the popular claim as fact.** "Orang tu betul. Makcik tak nafikan."
2. **Show the numbers that support it.** "49% masa untung besar. 58-71% masa normal."
3. **Pivot to the bigger problem.** "Tapi itu bukan cerita penuh."
4. **Reveal what the narrative hides.** "Gentari bakar RM1-1.5b/tahun tanpa pernah untung."

## Why This Works

- Reader feels heard (their belief is validated)
- Trust is built (Makcik agrees with them)
- The pivot lands harder (they didn't expect the twist)
- The real target is exposed (management accountability, not government extraction)

## Anti-Pattern vs Correct Pattern

**Anti-pattern:** "Kerajaan bukan makan PETRONAS" = defensive, reader disengages.
**Correct pattern:** "PETRONAS memang ATM. Tapi siapa jaga mesin tu?" = accepts framing, redirects to bigger problem.

## The "Dual-Lubang" Metaphor

Frame the problem as TWO simultaneous leaks, not one:
- One external (government dividend extraction)
- One internal (management capital inefficiency)

Reader sees the full picture. Both are real. Both happen simultaneously.

## Proven Example (2026-07-13)

PETRONAS ATM article evolution:
- **Version 1 (defensive):** "Kerajaan bukan makan — itu hak shareholder" → reader disengages
- **Version 2 (accepting):** "PETRONAS memang ATM. Tapi siapa jaga mesin tu?" → Gemini audit PASSED F2

## Pitfalls

- Don't fully reject the popular narrative — reader will disengage
- Don't fully confirm it either — that's just agreeing with the crowd
- The pivot must be DATA-DRIVEN, not opinion-driven
- The "bigger problem" must be more important than the original narrative
- Don't use the word "myth" — it implies the reader is stupid for believing it
- End with questions, not prescriptions — Makcik provokes, doesn't legislate
