## v2-specific voice patterns

### 1. Reader self-location opening
Bukan "Industry races on latency." Tapi "Siapa Anda Dalam Cerita Ni?" — paksa reader position diri dalam cerita. Format: 3 quick scenarios (email/report/recommendation) + "Mungkin hari ini pun ada."

### 2. The three-reason mechanical breakdown
Bukan "psychology of rubber-stamping." Tapi concrete 3 sebab yang reader boleh verify dalam diri:
- Sebab pertama: anda penat
- Sebab kedua: anda ada kerja lain
- Sebab ketiga: kalau reject, apa jadi?

Tiga sebab tu bukan analisis — tu *checklist* yang reader self-administer.

### 3. "Pattern Yang Anda Mungkin Tak Nampak"
Move from external critique → internal audit. List 5 places reader sudah jadi "human in the loop" tanpa sedar:
1. Sign form tanpa baca
2. Approve work yang orang lain hantar
3. Terima default setting
4. Trust recommendation system
5. Tekan "I agree" tanpa scroll

Setiap satu dengan concrete example. Bukan theory.

### 4. The Hostage Reframe
"Authority tanpa power = liability sponge." Konkrit: doktor sign 200 medical report sehari, auditor check 50 output sehari, compliance officer 30 saat satu. Bukan "tak cukup masa" — *survival mode* untuk overwhelmed professional.

**Frame formula:** "Tanggungjawab ada. Power takde. Itu definisi hostage."

### 5. Counter-pattern naming
Senarai 5 concrete things real safe systems buat:
1. Auto-reject yang obviously salah
2. Human hanya review yang uncertain
3. Audit trail automatic
4. Witness multiple channel
5. Floor yang tak penat

Bukan teori — bullets yang reader boleh check against their own system.

### 6. Three-question closer (audit, not summary)
Bukan generic conclusion. Tapi 3 soalan untuk self-audit:
- Satu: Kali terakhir fully reject AI recommendation — bila?
- Dua: Kalau system buat error, apa yang anda akan jawab?
- Tiga: Minggu ni, berapa kali tekan approve tanpa scroll?

Force introspection. Closing line: "Tutup Mata, Bukan Cancel" — keep the option open, don't demand immediate action.

### 7. The "Designed For It" Move
Paling Arif: "Anda tak salah sebab anda rubber stamp. Anda didesign untuk jadi macam tu." — Releases shame, but keeps awareness. Reader doesn't have to hate themselves, just notice the pattern.

## Lesson learned v1→v2

**When Arif says "pastikan manusia boleh relate"** — that means:
1. Drop the "industry" framing
2. Drop the technical abstraction
3. Use second-person ("anda") to force reader into the story
4. Make the audit checklist concrete (3 soalan, 5 patterns, 3 sebab)
5. Release shame before demanding awareness ("anda tak salah, anda didesign")
6. End with self-audit, not positioning demand

**When to switch modes:** v1 for industry positioning paper, v2 for mass-reach essay. Both have their place. The SKILL.md should encode that the agent offers both modes, not defaults to one.
