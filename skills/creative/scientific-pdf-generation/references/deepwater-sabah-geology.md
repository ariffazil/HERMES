## NSPW Mud Canopy (Morley et al., 2023, Geosphere 19(1), 291-334)

- **NOT igneous volcanism** — post-subduction, overpressure-driven fluid expulsion
- ~50 mud volcano centres, each 4–80 km²
- Total canopy area: ~1,900 km²
- Built during short burst in Middle Miocene (~10.5 Ma)
- Only second mud canopy system described globally — largest and most complex
- Directly controlled deposition of Rotan Sands turbidite reservoirs
- Causal chain: old subduction → thick gas-rich sediment wedge + fossil slab → collision/compression + faults → overpressure in shales → mud volcanoes

## Crustal Architecture (Continental, Not Oceanic)

- nBOSS: 46 broadband seismometers across Sabah (2018-2020)
- Crustal thickness: 24 km (central Sabah, drip-related extension) to 60 km (Crocker Range, DG underthrust)
- PSCS subduction terminated ~21 Ma (Hall, 2017)
- Fossil PSCS slab imaged as high-velocity mantle body
- NW Borneo Trough = flexural/gravity-driven, NOT active subduction
- Lithospheric drip from Semporna Peninsula (Pilia et al., 2023, Nature Geoscience)

## Reservoir Architecture — Pre-Kinematic vs Syn-Kinematic

| Characteristic | Pre-Kinematic | Syn-Kinematic |
|---|---|---|
| Deposition timing | Before anticline growth | During active fold growth |
| Sand geometry | Laterally extensive, sheet-like | Ponded in mini-basins |
| Lateral extent | >10 km | 2-5 km (confined) |
| Play type | Stratigraphic pinch-out | Structural + combination |
| Prediction | Seismic geomorphology | Growth strata analysis |

Key reservoirs: Kebabangan, Kinarut (pre-kinematic), Kamunsu (syn-kinematic).

## Petroleum System — Key Risks

- **Source:** Type II/III kerogen (terrestrial organic matter transported by MTCs). Mature source rock kitchens NOT well-defined — key geological risk (2025 assessment).
- **Reservoir:** Miocene turbiditic submarine fan systems. West Crocker Fm onshore analog = >20,000 km² sand-rich fan.
- **Seal:** Marine shales (top seal) + lateral seal across fault planes (Allan fault-plane mapping essential).
- **Trap/Charge:** Temporally coupled — oil expelled during early thrusting, re-migrates as crest positions shift, gas expelled post-peak thrusting. Northern structures → predominantly gas (more destructive phase).
