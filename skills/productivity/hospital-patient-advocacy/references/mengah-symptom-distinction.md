# Post-Op Symptom Interpretation: "Mengah" (BM)

The distinction between "mengah di dada" (chest tightness/breathlessness) and "mengah dekat perut" (abdominal bloating/tightness) is critical post-laparotomy. Patients often use "mengah" generically — always clarify the LOCATION.

## Decision Tree

| Patient Says | What It Likely Means | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **"Mengah dekat perut"** — tightness in abdomen after drinking | Post-op ileus (normal). Gut slow to wake, air/liquid trapped → abdominal distension pushing diaphragm. **Expected Day 1-4 post-laparotomy.** | Sip slowly, sit upright (Fowler's), monitor. Not a red flag. |
| **"Mengah di dada"** — breathlessness, can't catch breath | ⚠️ Possible aspiration, pleural effusion, atelectasis, or PE. **This IS a red flag.** | Stop oral intake immediately. Call nurse. Check SpO2. Elevate head. |
| **"Mengah" — unclear location** | Ambiguous. Could be either. | Ask: "Kat perut rasa ketat, atau kat dada rasa sesak nafas?" Then apply decision tree above. |

## Key Principle

**Never assume "mengah" = aspiration.** In post-laparotomy patients, abdominal distension → diaphragmatic pressure → "mengah" sensation is common and benign. But always rule out the dangerous form first by clarifying location.
