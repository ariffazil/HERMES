# NOC Shareholder Paradox & Fiscal Dependency

> **Purpose:** Reference for analyzing NOC dividend discourse and government fiscal dependency. Proven on PETRONAS 2026-07-13.

---

## The "Government Milks [NOC]" Narrative

Almost always wrong. Check these before accepting the narrative:

### 1. Payout Ratio History

| Scenario | Payout ratio | Cash pile | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative shareholder | 40-70% | Growing | NOT milking |
| Moderate shareholder | 60-80% | Stable | Normal |
| Aggressive shareholder | 80-100% | Declining | Milking |
| Extractive | >100% | Declining + borrowing | Destroying |

If payout is 40-70% and cash pile is growing, the government is CONSERVATIVE.

### 2. Total Contribution vs Dividend

Government receives MORE than just dividend:
- Dividend
- Petroleum income tax (PITA)
- Royalties
- Export duty
- State sales tax
- Cash payments (social contributions)

**Example PETRONAS FY2025:**
- Dividend: RM 32bn
- Total contribution: RM 67.6bn
- Ratio: 2.1x

PM framing "only 5-6% of revenue" cherry-picks dividend. Total = ~20% of federal revenue.

### 3. Subsidy Counter-Flow

If government spends MORE on fuel subsidies than it receives in dividends → the "extraction" narrative is backwards.

**Example PETRONAS FY2026:**
- Dividend received: RM 20bn
- Fuel subsidy cost: RM 38bn
- Subsidy/Dividend ratio: 1.9x

Government is PAYING OUT more to keep fuel cheap than it TAKES from PETRONAS.

### 4. The Sovereign Paradox

PETRONAS = 100% government-owned. Government = THE shareholder. Dividend = shareholder's right, not extraction.

The narrative "kerajaan milk PETRONAS" assumes SEPARATE entities with CONFLICTING interests. They're THE SAME ENTITY.

### 5. What "Milking" Actually Looks Like

If government was truly milking:
- Payout ratio 80-100% every year
- Never cut dividend even when PAT falls
- Force NOC to borrow to pay dividend
- Ignore capex needs to maximize short-term payout

Check: did ANY of these happen? If not, narrative is wrong.

## Output Format

When analyzing NOC-dividend discourse:

```
DIVIDEND ANALYSIS
  Payout ratio: X% (Y-year average)
  Cash pile: growing/declining
  Total contribution: RM Xbn (dividend + tax + payments)
  Subsidy counter-flow: RM Xbn/year
  Verdict: Conservative / Moderate / Aggressive / Extractive
```

## Provenance

- 2026-07-13: PETRONAS — 41-71% payout ratio, cash growing RM 15.9bn/year, dividend cut when needed. PM "5-6%" framing understates 19.7% total dependency. Fuel subsidies (RM 38bn) nearly 2x dividend (RM 20bn). Verdict: conservative shareholder, not extractor.
