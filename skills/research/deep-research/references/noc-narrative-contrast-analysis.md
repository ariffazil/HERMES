# NOC Narrative vs Reality Contrast Analysis

> **Purpose:** Structured method for contrasting state-owned enterprise (NOC/GLC) management narratives against actual financial numbers. Proven on PETRONAS FY2025 vs media releases (2026-07-13).

---

## When to Use

After building a financial projection for any NOC/GLC/sovereign entity, ALWAYS run this contrast analysis. The gap between narrative and numbers is often the most valuable insight.

## The 7-Claim Framework

Extract management's stated claims from media releases, IFR commentary, and CEO statements. Score each against actual numbers:

| Score | Meaning | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| ALIGNED ✓ | Narrative matches numbers | Claim supported by ratios, not just absolutes |
| PARTIALLY ALIGNED | True but misleading | Claim is technically correct but hides context |
| MIXED | Some truth, some spin | Real actions exist but outcomes contradict |
| MISLEADING | Narrative contradicts numbers | Key metric moves opposite to claim |
| DELIBERATELY OPAQUE | Data hidden | Critical metric never disclosed |

## Common Claim Patterns to Check

| Claim | What to check | Common trap |
|---|---|---|
| "Disciplined cost management" | Opex/Revenue RATIO (not absolutes) | Costs fell because revenue fell — that's math, not discipline |
| "Portfolio high-grading" | ROACE trend + gearing trend | If ROACE falls and gearing rises, portfolio got worse |
| "Record deliveries/volume" | Volume vs revenue vs PAT divergence | Volume up but PAT down = margin compression |
| "Energy transition progress" | Transition arm standalone PAT | If PAT never disclosed, "progress" is PR, not finance |
| "Delivering shareholder value" | Dividend policy vs earnings trend | Maintaining dividend from falling earnings ≠ value creation |
| "Strengthening resilience" | ROACE, gearing, production trend | If all three worsen, "resilience" is narrative, not reality |
| "Strategic partnerships" | Actual deal terms disclosed? | "Partnership" without terms = announcement, not transaction |

## The Shareholder Paradox (NOC-specific)

When public discourse says "government milks [NOC]":

1. **Check payout ratio history.** If 40-70% and cash pile growing → conservative shareholder, not extractor.
2. **Check total contribution vs dividend.** Government often receives 2-3x dividend in total (tax + royalties + payments). "Only 5% of revenue" framing cherry-picks dividend.
3. **Check subsidy counter-flow.** If government spends MORE on fuel subsidies than it receives in dividends → the "extraction" narrative is backwards.
4. **The paradox:** Government IS the shareholder. Taking a dividend = exercising ownership rights, not extraction. Frame this explicitly.
