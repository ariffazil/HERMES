# AI Narrative Forensics — Detecting AI Mediation in Institutional Communications

## When to Use

When analyzing corporate, government, or institutional communications for evidence of AI mediation — not "did they type into ChatGPT," but "does the narrative architecture, lexical pattern, and conceptual blindspot map to known AI-output signatures?"

Triggers:
- CEO restructuring announcements with suspiciously clean frameworks
- Policy documents with tiered KPI structures but no causal logic
- Communications that feel "too structured" relative to the speaker's known style
- Closed-door events announcing public-domain information
- User asks "did they use AI for this?"

## Core Framework: Power-Law Median Alignment

AI does NOT treat genius and stupidity equally. AI **systematically favors stupidity over genius** because:

1. **Training data is power-law distributed** — a few ideas are high-frequency (bureaucratic templates, KPI frameworks, corporate jargon), most are low-frequency (field expertise, sovereignty-specific reasoning, tacit knowledge)
2. **Median alignment = peak of power-law** — AI aligns to what's *most written*, not what's *most true*
3. **Stupidity is in the head, genius in the tail** — bureaucratic prose dominates the corpus; nuanced causal reasoning is rare

**Implication:** When an institutional communication uses language that perfectly matches the "median" of global corporate/policy writing, that IS the AI signal — not an anomaly.

## Five AI Fingerprint Signatures

### 1. Lexical Pattern Convergence (Strongest Surface Signal)
- Repeated use of the same 6-8 corporate adjectives across all communications
- Words like "agile, competitive, future-ready, resilient, streamlined, data-driven"
- These are GPT-4/Claude default outputs for "write a restructuring announcement"
- **Check:** Search the CEO's historical communications. If these words appeared suddenly in the last 12-18 months, AI influence is high.

### 2. Tiered Framework Without Causal Logic (Medium Signal)
- Clean structural differentiation: "3 phases, 5 pillars, 7 levers"
- Each tier has a role but no causal explanation of WHY this structure solves the problem
- Output: "Strategy Phase 1 → Phase 2 → Phase 3" without evidence of WHY these phases
- **Check:** Ask "what problem does Phase 2 solve that Phase 1 cannot?" If no answer exists, the structure is decorative.

### 3. Confidence-Competence Gap (Medium Signal)
- Uniformly confident language: "essential," "indispensable," "foundational"
- Zero admission of uncertainty, no caveats, no "we believe," "initial results suggest"
- AI amplifies confidence because it optimizes for fluency, not epistemic integrity
- **Check:** Count uncertainty markers ("may," "could," "preliminary," "we are testing"). <3 in a major announcement = suspicious.

### 4. Blindspot Negative Space (Strongest Deep Signal — Highest Specificity)
- **This is the most diagnostic signal.** What is NOT said that a human in this speaker's position would naturally address?
- AI's blindspots are deterministic: anything low-frequency in training data is invisible
- For a Malaysian NOC CEO: Malay social contract, Petros-Sarawak dynamics, fiscal dependency, institutional memory
- **Check:** List the top 5 domain-specific issues the speaker SHOULD address. Count how many appear. If 0-1 appear, AI mediation is very likely — these are things the corpus doesn't contain.

### 5. Circular Justification (Medium Signal)
- "We are removing unnecessary work" → What is unnecessary? → "Work that we removed."
- The output defines the input. No external verification criteria.
- **Check:** Can the justification be tested against an external metric? If no, it's internally self-referential — an AI pattern.

## The Closed-Door Town Hall Paradox (Diagnostic Technique)

When an institution holds a "closed-door" or "private" town hall to announce content that is subsequently reported in mainstream media on the same day:

**Step 1:** List every item announced.
**Step 2:** Classify each as PUBLIC (subsequently reported), TRADE-SECRET (commercially sensitive), or ACTUALLY-SECRET (never leaked).
**Step 3:** If 90%+ items are PUBLIC, the closed-door format was a **performance of secrecy** — manufacturing executive gravity, not protecting information.

**Five hypotheses to evaluate (ranked by probability):**

| H# | Hypothesis | Typical P | Indicator |
|----|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| H1 | Performance of secrecy — manufacturing gravitas | 0.40-0.50 | Content was public-ready; format manufactured insider feel |
| H2 | AI-mediated script — controlled delivery to hide artifice | 0.25-0.35 | Script structure is suspiciously clean; Q&A was curated |
| H3 | Genuine HR practice — internal-first communication | 0.10-0.20 | Standard HR protocol; announcement was genuinely sensitive to employees |
| H4 | Legal risk management — limited discovery risk | 0.05-0.10 | Separation negotiations ongoing; ambiguity protects the company |
| H5 | Actual secrets discussed | 0.02-0.05 | Content NOT in subsequent media; specific names/numbers leaked |

**H1+H2 together = 65-85% probability.** The closed door serves dual purpose: performance + artifice shield.

## Narrative vs Reality Contrast (Verification Technique)

After identifying the narrative, construct a contrast table:

| CEO Said | Reality Check | Score |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| Quote 1 | What the numbers actually show | ALIGNED / PARTIALLY / MISLEADING / OPAQUE |
| Quote 2 | ... | ... |

**Key:** The contrast table IS the credibility anchor. Without it, you're analyzing narrative, not reality.

**Common traps:**
- "Not retrenchment" when 5,000 jobs are eliminated (MISLEADING)
- "Removing unnecessary work" without defining "unnecessary" (UNVERIFIED)
- "Agile, competitive, future-ready" — these are power-law tokens, not strategy (AI ARTIFACT)
