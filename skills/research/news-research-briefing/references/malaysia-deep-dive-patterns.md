# Malaysia Deep-Dive Intelligence Patterns

## Proven Source Hierarchy (tested July 2026)

### Macro Data Sources (ranked by reliability)
1. **Bank Negara Malaysia (bnm.gov.my)** — OPR, inflation, GDP forecast, financial stability report. OBS.
2. **Department of Statistics Malaysia (dosm.gov.my)** — CPI, GDP, trade, employment. OBS.
3. **Trading Economics** — Ringgit rate, bond yields, quick macro snapshots. OBS/DER.
4. **Bloomberg/Reuters** — GDP beats, trade data, market reactions. OBS. (web_extract works on Reuters, Bloomberg paywalled)
5. **World Bank / IMF** — Country forecasts, structural assessments. DER.

### Political Intelligence Sources
1. **East Asia Forum (eastasiaforum.org)** — Peer-reviewed political analysis. Excellent for election dynamics, coalition analysis. Full text extractable.
2. **SCMP (scmp.com)** — Southeast Asia political coverage. Good for Malaysia-specific election reporting. web_extract works.
3. **ISEAS Yusof Ishak Institute (iseas.edu.sg)** — Academic-grade political + economic analysis. PDF available for most perspectives.
4. **Fulcrum.sg** — ISEAS commentary site. Shorter, punchier political analysis. web_extract works.
5. **RSIS (rsis.edu.sg)** — Singapore-based strategic analysis. Good for snap polls, security dynamics.
6. **The Economist** — Best high-level framing ("two state elections may break Malaysia's ruling coalition"). Paywalled — use web_search for summary.
7. **Fair Observer** — Longer-form political analysis. web_extract works.

### Social Fabric & Structural Sources
1. **EMIR Research (emirresearch.com)** — Best Malaysian think tank for social policy analysis. "Budget 2026 and the Squeezed Middle" = definitive M40 analysis.
2. **Khazanah Research Institute (krinstitute.org)** — KRI does deep structural analysis. Tariff implications, AI impact studies.
3. **TalentCorp** — Brain drain data, workforce impact studies.
4. **Penang Institute** — Education, STEM, PISA analysis.
5. **NST / The Star / FMT** — Rakyat-level reporting. Cost of living, housing, subsidy impact.
6. **Social media (Instagram viral reels)** — Surprisingly useful for rakyat sentiment. "Middle class squeeze" content = ground truth of felt experience.

### AI & Workforce Sources
1. **ISEAS Perspective 2026/41** — "Augmentation or Elimination: AI Impact on Malaysian Economy" = definitive. 680K jobs at risk, +2-3% unemployment under stress.
2. **PwC AI Jobs Barometer 2026** — Two-track labour market global data.
3. **Gotchaa Lab blog** — Best practical analysis of AI impact on Malaysian tech workers. The "paradox" framing (tech boom + tech layoffs simultaneously) is accurate.
4. **NAIO (ai.gov.my)** — Government AI policy. AI Technology Action Plan 2026-2030.
5. **Anthropic labour market study** — 14% hiring drop for 22-25 year olds in AI-exposed occupations.

## Key Analytical Frameworks for Malaysia

### Acemoglu Extractive Institutions Lens
- Subsidies benefit cronies more than rakyat (extractive design)
- Middle income trap = institutional failure, not just economic
- Brain drain = symptom of non-meritocratic system
- GDP growth without felt prosperity = extractive growth pattern

### Calhoun Universe 25 Lens
- Phase B→C transition: "beautiful mouse" — stable appearance, internal withdrawal
- Institutional resistance to reform = coalition survival > policy quality
- Vitality squeeze: workers (rakyat, PETRONAS staff) getting more tired, decision-makers getting more distant
- Youth withdrawal (not rebellion) = social media escape, mental health decline, 28% planning exit

## Pitfalls (from this session)

- **Don't just aggregate news.** "What's brewing" requests want the SUBTEXT, not just headlines.
- **Don't forget PETRONAS angle.** For Arif, PETRONAS is always relevant — rightsizing, dividend pressure, fiscal dependency.
- **Don't smooth contradictions.** "GDP up but rakyat suffering" IS the story. Don't resolve the tension — highlight it.
- **Don't overclaim on political predictions.** Label election analysis as INT/SPEC, not OBS.
- **Use parallel delegation.** 3 subagents × 6 calls = 18 data points in 2 minutes vs 6 minutes serial.
- **Ringgit weakness matters.** Always include latest rate — it's the felt indicator rakyat track daily.
- **Subsidy reform = political bomb.** Never treat it as just fiscal policy — it's the intersection of economics, politics, and social contract.
