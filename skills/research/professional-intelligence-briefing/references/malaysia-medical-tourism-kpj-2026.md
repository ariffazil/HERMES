## Critical Analysis (INT — Murray Hunter, The Vibes, Apr 2026)

1. **Data inflation:** MHTC counts ANY foreign passport holder (expats, foreign workers, tourists). 65% are Indonesian — many are resident workers, not flying in for treatment.
2. **Revenue growth is inflation, not demand:** RM2.7B→RM3.3B = 23% jump, but 15% medical inflation + 6% SST on foreign patients → real volume growth near zero.
3. **RM2,000/patient average:** suggests bulk is low-value outpatient, not high-value surgery narrative.
4. **Rogue doctors & agents:** some bypass MHTC system, use private agents, cut out hospitals.
5. **Malpractice gap:** no international patient insurance. Federal Court ruled patients have no right of appeal if MMC clears the doctor.
6. **Weak marketing:** Thailand leverages tourism + wellness brand better.

### Risks

1. Medical inflation > volume growth
2. BOR 67% = capacity slack (2,200 more beds coming)
3. P/E 39x = premium, execution must deliver
4. RM4-5B capex = gearing risk
5. Indonesia dependency (currency + policy risk)
6. Competition intensifying (IHH, Sunway, Thomson)
7. Workforce pressure (doctor/nurse shortage)

### Investment Thesis (INT)

**Bull:** Largest private hospital network MY, KPJHS integration, Mayo Clinic partnership, RM4-5B capex → revenue step-change by 2028-2030, AI/genomics forward-looking, GLC backing, Indonesia structural demand.

**Bear:** P/E 39x expensive, execution risk on massive capex, BOR has room to fill before expanding, medical tourism revenue per patient low, dividend yield minimal.

**Catalyst watch:** Brownfield expansion completion (2027-2028), BOR improvement, KPJHS integration milestones.
