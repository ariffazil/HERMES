## Two Exit Pathways

| | MSS (Voluntary) | Rating 4 → PIP → Termination |
|---|---|---|
| Who initiates | Employee applies OR PETRONAS approaches via "Exploratory Discussion" | Management/PDC assigns rating |
| Package | "Market-competitive, exceeds statutory minimum" (official) | Notice pay only (statutory minimum) |
| Formula (Lowyat) | `Last drawn salary × years of service × 1.5 + 4 months` | Statutory: 20 days/year for 5+ years service |
| Optics | Clean exit, neutral/positive reference | "Poor performance" — complicated reference |
| Timeline | Negotiation-based | 6-12 months (PIP → extension → termination) |
| Tax | Lump sum taxable — maxes out bracket for the year | Notice pay taxable |

## Rating System (OFR — Overall Final Rating)

| Rating | Meaning | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Outstanding | Safe |
| 2 | Exceeds expectations | Safe |
| 3H | High "Meets expectations" | Safe |
| 3M | Medium "Meets expectations" | Safe |
| 3L | Low "Meets most base targets" | Precursor to PIP |
| 4 | Below Expectation | **Danger zone** — triggers mandatory PIP |
| 5 | Worst (old M3 system) | Terminal |

**Bell curve / forced ranking:** 20% high performers, 70% average (3H/3M/3L), 10% below. Bottom 10% automatically subject to PIP.

Source: LinkedIn discussions, Industrial Court cases, M3 system documentation.

## The PIP Process at PETRONAS

1. Low OFR rating (3L or 4) triggers mandatory PIP placement
2. First PIP: 6 months with specific KPIs, monthly performance reviews
3. Support provided: coaching, mentoring, additional technical resources
4. If no improvement: PIP extended (typically 3 more months)
5. If still failing: Employment terminated with notice pay
6. PDC (People Development Committee) makes all decisions — NOT a single manager
7. Employee's self-rating in EPM is NOT the final rating — PDC decides

## Legal Cases (Industrial Court — HARD EVIDENCE)

### Nazreen Begum v PETRONAS (Award 1614, 2018)
- Rating "4" in FY2013 OFR
- 6-month PIP starting March 2014
- PIP extended 3 months (total 9 months)
- Still rated "4" → Terminated 16 March 2015
- **Court upheld dismissal as "just cause and excuse"**

### Syed Mohd Azinuddin v PETRONAS (Award 667, 2026)
- Executive since 2007, sustained "3L" ratings
- Offered voluntary separation — **DECLINED**
- First PIP (2020) → still "3L"
- Second PIP (June-Nov 2021) with additional support
- Terminated 16 March 2022
- **Court upheld dismissal**
- Key quote: *"The law does not require an employer to indefinitely subsidize underperformance."*

### Norain Redzkiah v PETRONAS (2019)
- 24 years service, dismissed after PIP
- Awarded RM1.13M for wrongful dismissal
- **PETRONAS won appeal — award reversed**

### B. Braun v Mugunthan (Federal Court, 2024)
- Even if employee signed MSS voluntarily, court can examine whether **indirect pressure** was applied
- If employer offered "MSS with higher package" vs "dismissal with lower package" = coercion = MSS invalid
- **This protects Arif** if PETRONAS uses Rating 4 to force worse terms

## Strategic Risk Pattern

If MSS is pending AND Rating 4 is assigned:
1. PETRONAS can argue separation is for **poor performance** (not voluntary) → smaller package
2. Or use low rating to **justify denying MSS** and proceed with managed exit on worse terms
3. Rating 4 creates documentary support for "poor performance" termination
