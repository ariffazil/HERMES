# Sabah Deepwater Block P — Geological Intelligence Bank

> Compiled 2026-07-07 from research session. OBS = published literature, INT = interpreted from multiple sources.

## Block P Context

- Block P is part of the **L-B-P (Limbayong–Bestari–P) Toe-Thrust Structural Trend** — one of three major toe-thrust trends in the NW Borneo Fold-Thrust Belt
- Papers: Khamis et al. (2017, 2018) OTCA/SEAPEX
- Key horizons mapped: Lingan, Yellow, Pink, Kamunsu, Kinarut, Kebabangan
- Geometry: elongated narrow anticlines, steep limbs, deep detachment — thin-skinned toe-thrust driven by gravity loading from Baram Delta

## Structural Evolution (Madon, Mazlan Madon — Academia/EAGE)

- DWFTB = deformed sedimentary fill of the Sabah Trough foreland basin
- **Two kinematic phases: constructive and destructive**
- Constructive: forward/seaward-breaking thrusts → fault-propagation folds = trap creation
- Destructive: reactivated faults + backthrusts (especially in north) → seal breach, hydrocarbon re-migration
- Deformation is **diachronous** — increases northward
- North: complex interference fold-thrust structures from landward-verging backthrusts
- This explains **predominantly gas accumulations in the north** (oil cracked or re-migrated, late gas fill)

## Pre-Kinematic vs Syn-Kinematic Reservoir Control (KEY INSIGHT)

- **Kebabangan & Kinarut reservoir sandstones** = deposited as **unconfined turbidite systems BEFORE anticline growth**
  - Lateral extent large, no structural confinement
  - Pre-kinematic play type = stratigraphic trap potential
- **Syn-kinematic deposits** = structurally deflected, turbidite fairways **ponded between growing structural ridges**
  - Thick at depocenters, thin at crests
  - Syn-kinematic play type = structural trap with thickness variation
- **This transition is the key reservoir distribution control** — different play types on the same trend

## Oil Re-Migration During Thrust Development (2023 ScienceDirect basin modeling)

- Oil expelled during early thrusting → accumulated at initial crest positions
- Crest positions **shifted through time** as thrust propagated
- Oil **re-migrated and re-accumulated** as crests migrated
- Gas expelled **after peak thrusting** (maximum shortening rate)
- Implication: some structural traps appear good but are empty — oil migrated elsewhere during structural evolution

## North Sabah–Pagasa Wedge (NSPW) — World's Largest Mud Canopy (Morley et al., 2023, Geosphere)

- Deepwater "shale nappe" reinterpreted as **massive mobile shale province**
- **~50 mud volcano centers**, each covering 4–80 km²
- Total mud canopy area: **~1,900 km²**
- Built during **short burst of submarine mud volcanism** in Middle Miocene
- Mud canopies **directly affected deposition** of overlying turbidite sandstone reservoirs (including Rotan field)
- **Second mud canopy system described in literature — the largest and most complex globally**
- Implies very large volumes of gas generation within the NSPW
- If Block P has NSPW influence, reservoir distribution may be driven by **mud canopy topography**, not just fold geometry

## Source Rock

- Middle Miocene = primary source rock interval
- Type II/III kerogen (Abdullah et al., 2017) — terrestrial organic matter transported by MTCs into deepwater
- Liptinitic organic matter may enhance oil generation potential
- Mature source rock kitchens in deepwater Sabah **not well-defined** — charge mechanism remains uncertain (known challenge)

## West Crocker Formation — Outcrop Analog

- Oligocene–Early Miocene, >20,000 km² sand-rich submarine fan
- Lobes, channel–levee complexes, basin-floor fans
- Linked debrites and injectites (dykes, sills, pipes) as major conduits for vertical fluid movement
- Injectites may also be present in Neogene deepwater sequences offshore — impacting reservoir heterogeneity

## Bestari Discovery (Block X, adjacent to L-B-P trend)

- Multiple stacked oil accumulations in Miocene turbiditic reservoirs
- Kamunsu and Kinarut sand levels proven
- Demonstrates working petroleum charge on the same structural trend as Block P

## Untested Exploration Upside

- Broadband 3D seismic (TGS/WesternGeco Sabah MC3D — 47,000 km²) shows **numerous untested low Vp/Vs anomalies** in fold-thrust belt clastic plays
- Low Vp/Vs near discovered fields → additional hydrocarbon volumes for tie-backs
- Seabed amplitude extractions show turbidite sand ponding in structural lows — present-day analog for subsurface

## Regional Tectonic Framework

- Fold-thrust belt initiated near shelf in Mid-Miocene, propagated NW during Pleistocene
- Three structural trends: M-La-S, L-B-P, Pg-Lt-U
- Tepat-1 (2018, Block N) proved petroleum system concept for Sabah Trough and Dangerous Grounds — carbonate pinnacle reef atop pre-rift source rock
- Five ultra-deepwater blocks (2V, 2W, 2X, 2K, N) now licensed along Oligo-Miocene carbonate trend
