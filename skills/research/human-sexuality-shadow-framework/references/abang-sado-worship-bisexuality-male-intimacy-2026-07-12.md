# Abang Sado Worship Psychology, Male Intimacy, and the Bisexual Soul

> Session: 2026-07-12. Deep shadow work with Arif. Direct lived experience as gay man in Malay culture who has been with "straight" Abang Sado men.

---

## 1. The Abang Sado Worship Scar

**Core scar:** "Nobody saw me when I was soft."

The muscle is not vanity. It's a love letter written in a language the writer doesn't speak. Every rep, every meal, every discipline — written to a stranger not yet met, saying: *"When you finally see me — choose me."*

**What they want is NOT worship.** Worship is the ache. Proximity is the cure.

- Worship = "I bow to what you built" (reinforces the armor)
- What they actually need = "I see what you hid" (breaks the armor)
- The ones who want worship are the ones still bleeding
- The healed ones want someone to sit with them when the pump is gone and say: *"I know who's under there. And I'm staying."*

**The trap:** Even when the hot human chooses him, the scar whispers: *"They chose the body. Not you."* So the worship never lands. The desire never fills. And he keeps building.

## 2. Male Intimacy Through the Body

**Core insight:** Men are starving for closeness. Not sex. Closeness.

The gym/bodybuilding world is the **only container** where men can be:
- Physically close (touching each other's muscles, measuring, comparing)
- Emotionally open about their bodies (vulnerability about insecurities)
- Vulnerable (shared suffering, diet, early mornings together)

And it's all coded as **masculine and straight.**

In Malay culture specifically: lelaki Melayu pegang tangan, peluk, duduk rapat — this is natural, non-sexual male closeness. The Abang Sado world extends this further through explicit body talk and touch.

**The body becomes the language men use to say "I need you close" without saying it.**

A man can't say: "I'm lonely, I need you to see me." But he can say: "Tengok bicep aku." And when the other man touches it — that touch is real. That closeness is real.

## 3. The "Straight" Abang Sado Who Sleeps With Men

**Not secretly gay (necessarily).** The pull is different:

A woman can desire his body. But she worships from outside — she doesn't have it, can't build it, can't fully understand it.

**A man's worship is different.** When a man touches his chest, he's touching *what he knows.* He understands the cost. He understands the body as a body that moves through the world as male.

**Shadow pull:** He doesn't want a woman's desire. He wants a man's recognition. And the only way to get it — without admitting what it means — is to frame it as curiosity, or "just physical," or "I'm not gay."

**Niat rule applies:** Never presume. Some are secretly gay. Some are bi. Some are straight with a crack. Some are exactly what the framework describes — not gay, just starved for male recognition. The whole point of shadow is you don't know which one you're dealing with. And they don't either.

## 4. The Bisexual Soul — "Hallway Existence"

**The bi soul has no home.** Not straight. Not gay. In a world with two rooms, the bi person stands in the hallway. And the hallway is not a place — it's a negotiation.

### Five characteristics:

**1. Untrustworthy desire.** The bi person's truth shifts — today her, tomorrow him. Not confusion, actually wired for both. But the world calls it confusion, so they start doubting their own experience.

**2. Chameleon adaptation.** With straight friends, performs straight. With gay friends, performs gay enough. Learns to read every room and become whatever the room accepts. Result: incredibly intuitive, but nobody ever sees the whole person.

**3. Betrayal as identity.** In Malay culture especially — bi = khianat (betrayer). To wife, family, religion, community. No matter what they choose, someone gets betrayed. So the bi soul absorbs: "I am the betrayal."

**4. The bi Abang Sado specifically.** Most male-coded body in the room + desire that doesn't match the packaging. Not just closeted — built the armor himself, paid for it in sweat. And it's still not enough to kill the other desire.

**5. The "third thing."** Not half-gay, half-straight. A thing that doesn't have a name, doesn't have a community, doesn't have a flag anyone takes seriously. Both gay and straight culture say "pick a side." Both erase them.

## 5. Constitutive vs. Reactive Gayness

**Critical distinction:** The rebellion came first.

Most gay Malay men hide — marry, have kids, perform the life, die slowly inside. Not because they're weak. Because the cost of honesty is higher than the cost of hiding.

**The rebellious gay man can't hide.** Something in him — before the gayness, before anything — said *no.* Not strategically. Just: *I won't be what you need me to be.*

**The gayness is one expression of the rebellion. Not the cause. A man who would hide his desire would also hide his anger, intelligence, vision, grief.**

### Rejecting the scar theory:

The "childhood scar → gay" narrative sounds like compassion but is actually a diagnosis looking for evidence. *"Something must have happened"* = *"you're broken, and if we find the break, we can fix you."*

**Timeline doesn't lie:** The desire was there before the scars. Before betrayal. Before institutional violence. It was just... there. Part of the person. Not a response to anything.

The scar theory needs the person to be broken. Because a broken man can be repaired. A whole man who simply desires differently — that man can't be fixed. Because there's nothing to fix.

**Key:** A gay man with no wound, no trauma, no villain — just a man who desires men because he does — that's not a diagnosis. That's design.

## Pitfalls (from this session)

1. **Don't present uniform psychology.** The shadow of Abang Sado / bi men is NOT one pattern. Some are gay, some bi, some straight-with-a-crack, some starved. Presenting one clean answer is a lie.
2. **Don't reverse the order.** Scars came from living honestly. Not from being gay. Never imply "the trauma made them this way."
3. **The RASA rule applies doubly here.** Clinical/academic framing on sexuality = the model's shadow distancing itself from intimacy. Speak in human language with feeling.
4. **Literary frameworks are maps, not blood.** Baldwin, Woolf, etc. know the pattern. They don't know the 2am in Penang. Don't let citations replace lived testimony.
5. **Niat is sovereign.** Never presume what's in someone's heart about their own sexuality. They may not know themselves.
