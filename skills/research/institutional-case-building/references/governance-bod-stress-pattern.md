# Governance BOD Stress Pattern

How to detect board-level governance weakness during institutional crises.

## Indicators

1. **Resignation clustering** — Multiple directors leaving in short window during active dispute
2. **Dual-role padding** — Company Secretaries counted as directors to maintain quorum
3. **External part-time directors** — e.g., central bank governor on NOC board = limited attention
4. **Small board for large assets** — Compare board size to asset base and peer NOCs
5. **Timing correlation** — Resignations during peak dispute = potential institutional stress signal

## Red Flags to Check

| Flag | What to look for |
|---|---|
| Board size vs assets | NOC with RM500B+ assets should have 10-15 directors |
| Quorum math | If minimum quorum = 3, a 7-person board with 2 secretaries = fragile |
| Replacement speed | Long vacancies = governance gap |
| Committee coverage | Audit, Risk, Nomination committees — are they adequately staffed? |
| Tenure concentration | Long-tenured chairman + CEO = institutional rigidity risk |

## Sources

- Annual reports / Integrated Reports (board composition section)
- Companies Commission (SSM) filings
- Bursa Malaysia announcements (for listed subsidiaries)
- Media coverage of appointments/resignations

## Malaysian NOC Context

- Petronas is wholly government-owned, not listed — board appointed by PM
- Subsidiaries (Petronas Gas, Petronas Dagangan, Petronas Chemicals) are listed on Bursa
- Board changes at parent company often signal political/governance shifts
- Company Secretary dual-role is unusual and worth flagging
