# Institutional Collapse Spiral — Reference Pattern

**Canonical example:** Petronas 2024-2026 (rightsizing → espionage → Shell MDS → MBR freeze → governance erosion)

**When to load:** When analysis reveals multiple stress dimensions feeding each other. When user says "spiral," "death spiral," "cascade," "everything connected," or "simulative."

---

## The Pattern

Institutional collapse rarely comes from one event. It comes from a **feedback loop** where each stress dimension makes the others worse:

```
Financial stress (profit drop -21%, -32%)
    ↓
Cost cutting (10% rightsizing, workforce reduction)
    ↓
Governance erosion (BOD resignations, loyalty loss, knowledge drain)
    ↓
Intelligence compromise (data leaks, personnel exits to competitors)
    ↓
External exploitation (counterparties exploit weakness via legal instruments)
    ↓
More financial stress (payment freezes, litigation costs, lost revenue)
    ↓
More cost cutting → repeat
```

**Key diagnostic:** Spiral ≠ just multiple problems. Spiral = each problem makes the others worse.

## Petronas Case Study (2024-2026)

### Financial Dimension
- Profit: -21% (2023), -32% (2024) — consecutive major declines
- Response: 10% workforce rightsizing announced mid-2025
- Shell MDS: ~RM1.2B cash flow frozen (14 months × ~RM80M/month)

### Governance Dimension
- BOD: 3 resignations in 15 weeks (Oct 2024 – Jan 2025)
- Board size: 7 directors (2 are Company Secretaries) for RM500B+ asset
- Industry norm for NOCs: 10-15 directors
- Replacement: partial (1 new NED + 1 new Company Secretary)

### Workforce Dimension
- 10% rightsizing = ~5,000+ jobs cut
- BU Performance Manager (Khairul Akmal) applied to Petros Jan 2024, leaked data Jun 2024
- Signal: personnel with sensitive access exploring exits during stress period

### Legal Dimension
- Shell MDS interpleader (Nov 2024) — first time Shell sued Petronas in 60+ years
- Petros BG suit (Oct 2024) — "unconscionable" challenge
- Federal Court PDA vs DGO (Jan 2026) — constitutional question unresolved
- 3 simultaneous legal fronts during governance weakness

### External Exploitation Dimension
- Shell: "simulative neutral" — freeze payments, keep receiving gas, claim caught in middle
- Petros: signed competing GSA with Shell (Aug 2024) during federal-state tension
- Sarawak: DGO challenge to Petronas operating rights

### MBR Freeze (Consequence)
- MBR 2025: ZERO Sarawak blocks offered (first time in recent history)
- Shell absent from Sarawak upstream (not by choice — no blocks offered)
- Exploration licensing paused for full bid round cycle
- MBR 2026: Sarawak blocks return after CoA clarification

## Simulative Exploitation Framework

**Acemoglu's model:** Extractive institutions → internal decay → collapse (endogenous)

**Extended model:** Institution weak → external actors exploit → collapse accelerates (exogenous predation)

| Signal | Example |
|---|---|
| First-ever legal action at moment of weakness | Shell interpleader — first lawsuit vs Petronas in 60+ years |
| "Neutral party" posture while benefiting | Shell: "caught in middle" while saving ~RM1B cash flow |
| Historical absence of conflict | Shell-Petronas: decades of cooperation, zero litigation |
| Timing correlation with governance weakness | All actions during BOD thin period (7 people, 3 resignations) |
| Target bears deprivation cost | Petronas: supply gas 14 months without payment |

**Labeling:** INT (interpreted from behavioral pattern). Not CLAIM without direct evidence of intent.

## Detection Methodology

1. **Map stress indicators** across 5 dimensions over time
2. **Check linearity:** Is decline steady (LINEAR) or accelerating (SPIRAL)?
3. **Identify feedback loops:** Does dimension A worsen dimension B?
4. **Find weakest link:** Which dimension, if strengthened, breaks the loop?
5. **Identify trigger vs current state:** What started the spiral? Where is it now?

### Stress Index Components

| Component | Weight | Signals |
|---|---|---|
| Financial stress | 0.25 | Profit change, revenue change, cost cutting announced |
| Governance capacity | 0.25 | Board size, resignations, dual-role padding, tenure |
| Workforce stability | 0.20 | Rightsizing %, voluntary exits, key personnel departures |
| Legal exposure | 0.15 | Active litigation count, injunction value, regulatory uncertainty |
| External exploitation | 0.15 | Payment freezes, interpleader, competing claims |

**Composite stress index:** Weighted sum normalized to 0-1.
- 0.0-0.3: GREEN (stable)
- 0.3-0.5: YELLOW (stressed)
- 0.5-0.7: RED (critical)
- 0.7-1.0: CRITICAL (spiral likely)

## Connection to Acemoglu Critique

Acemoglu/Robinson (Why Nations Fail, 2012):
- Extractive institutions → elite capture → economic stagnation → collapse
- Cause: endogenous (institution destroys itself)

Simulative exploitation extension:
- Weak institutions → external actors exploit → accelerated collapse
- Cause: exogenous (institution destroyed by rational predators)
- Key: predator doesn't need to be "extractive" — just rational and opportunistic

**Why this matters for case building:**
When analyzing institutional collapse, don't just look at internal failures. Look at:
1. Who benefited from the institution's weakness?
2. Did they take unprecedented action at the moment of maximum weakness?
3. Did they use "neutral" legal posture while actually arbitraging chaos?
4. Is the institution bearing the cost of their "neutrality"?

If yes to all four → simulative exploitation, not just internal failure.
