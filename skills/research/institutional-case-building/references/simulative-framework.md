## The Gap in Acemoglu

Acemoglu & Robinson (2012) *Why Nations Fail*:
- **Extractive institutions** — elites capture rents from within → slow decay → collapse
- **Inclusive institutions** — broad participation → sustained growth

**What Acemoglu misses:** Institution collapse bukan hanya dari dalam. Boleh dari luar — bila external actor nampak institution tengah luka, dia exploit kelemahan tu. Bukan extract. Bukan inclusive. **Simulative.**

## Three Modes of Institutional Erosion

| Mode | Direction | Mechanism | Speed | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **EXTRACTIVE** | Inside → out | Elites capture rent, weaken productive base | Slow (years/decades) | 1MDB, PDVSA |
| **EXPLOITATIVE** | Outside → in | External actor takes advantage of weak governance | Medium (months) | Shell MDS interpleader |
| **SIMULATIVE** | Outside → in, disguised as neutral | External actor role-plays "caught in the middle" while arbitraging institutional chaos | Fast (weeks/months) | Shell MDS — "we're just neutral" while enjoying RM1B free gas for 14 months |

## Detection Signals

1. Actor uses "neutral party" legal posture (interpleader, injunction) while actually arbitraging institutional chaos
2. Actor takes legal action for the FIRST TIME in decades — at the exact moment of maximum institutional weakness
3. Actor benefits from delay (cash flow savings, free supply, etc.) while institution bears deprivation cost
4. Historical precedent: no prior litigation → sudden litigation during institutional stress = calculated, not reactive

## Key Diagnostic Question

**"Would this actor have done this when the institution was at full strength?"**

If NO → simulative.
