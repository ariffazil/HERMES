## Economics Theory (for explaining to Abang Sado)

### 1. Choice Paradox (Barry Schwartz)
Too many similar options → decision paralysis. Experiment: 24 jam flavors = 3% conversion. 6 flavors = 30% conversion. Customer freezes, queue builds, people leave.

### 2. Cannibalization
5 types of nasi lemak on the same table don't attract NEW customers. They split the SAME customers into smaller fragments. They compete with each other, not with the shop next door.

### 3. Pareto Principle (80/20)
80% of sales come from 2-3 top nasi lemak variants. The rest are visual decoration but carry real costs — extra ingredients, extra prep, extra spoilage risk.

### 4. Complexity Cost
Every additional variant = one extra sambal type + one extra lauk + one extra spoilage risk + one extra prep step. Invisible cost vendors don't see because they think "more choice = better."

### 5. Opportunity Cost
Table space is finite. 5 slots filled with all nasi lemak = zero slots for complementary products (cucur udang, karipap, murtabak mini) that attract DIFFERENT customers.

**Core insight:** 3 powerful types > 10 mediocre types.

## Vendor Communication Framework (5-Step)

Abang Sado can explain to a vendor with too many similar items:

| Step | What | Example Phrase |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Timing** | Come during slow hours, BUY first | (after purchase) |
| **2. Praise first** | Lead with compliment | "Abang, nasi lemak abang sedap. Tapi saya perasan..." |
| **3. Ask, don't order** | Question, not instruction | "Abang tau tak yang mana paling laku?" |
| **4. Suggest test** | One-week trial, not permanent | "Cuba seminggu je. Kalau tak jadi, undo je." |
| **5. Frame as partnership** | Side the vendor | "Saya beli hari-hari, saya nak abang maju." |

**Key principles:**
- Never sound like a consultant. Sound like a regular who cares.
- Always give an exit strategy ("kalau tak jadi, balik cara lama")
- Frame as "replace slow items with NEW items" not "remove things"
- Remove risk — people fear loss more than they value gain
