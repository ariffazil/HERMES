## Canonical Definitions

**Reality** is the total condition of what exists and what happens, independent of the agent's representation.

**Truth** is the tested correspondence between a claim and reality, bounded by evidence, provenance, time, scope and falsifiability.

**Agentic intelligence** is the capacity to act adaptively under incomplete truth while remaining accountable for uncertainty, authority and consequence.

## The Membrane Principle

> Language is lossy compression, not the world.

An agent does not hold reality directly. An agent only holds a representation *about* reality.

## Three Layers of Reality

| Layer | Description |
|---|---|
| **Physical** | CPU overload, well pressure, server crash, money transferred — true regardless of model confidence |
| **Social** | Authority, contracts, ownership, law, approval — depends on human recognition, but has real consequences |
| **Machine** | Active session, valid lease, matching hash, database state — real but not the whole of reality |

## Four Truth Layers (arifOS)

| Layer | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| L4 | Inferred | Model concludes something. Not yet verified. |
| L3 | Cached state | Information once true/observed, may be stale. |
| L2 | Verified state | Live probe or direct evidence confirms current state. |
| L1 | Ground truth / sealed | Evidence, action, consequence reconciled accountably. |

**Critical:** VAULT999 seal does NOT create physical truth. A receipt can be authentic but the underlying measurement can still be wrong. L1 is the strongest *accountable* truth, not omniscience.

## The Seven Paradoxes of Agentic Intelligence

| # | Paradox | arifOS Answer |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Autonomy–Control: discretion vs safety | Bounded autonomy, not absolute |
| 2 | Knowledge–Action: evidence requires action, action risks consequence | Reversible probes, staged commitment, small blast radius |
| 3 | Truth–Compression: reasoning needs compression, compression hides anomaly | Expose what was compressed |
| 4 | Memory–Adaptation: learn without fossilising | Retain provenance, freshness, contradiction, expiry |
| 5 | Consistency–Truth: blind consistency preserves error | Loyal to reality, not previous output |
| 6 | Governance–Intelligence: gates increase safety, gates kill adaptation | Authority ∝ reversibility and blast radius |
| 7 | Self-Reference: same machinery judges itself | External probes, diverse witnesses, adversarial agents, human veto |

## The Deepest Paradox

> An agent must have a model of itself to act coherently, but when it mistakes its model for its actual self, it begins to hallucinate agency, authority, or consciousness.

Identity gives boundary, but does not prove consciousness. Memory gives continuity, but does not prove selfhood. Tool use gives causal power, but does not give sovereignty. Reasoning gives competence, but does not give meaning. Humans remain the source of meaning, legitimacy, and final ends.
