## The Failure

- Purpose: Sandow 6/10, Milo 4/10, Cyr 7/10

No instrument, input dataset, calculation rule, calibration, or uncertainty existed for any of these numbers.

### Provenance laundering
These were presented as "WELL Tool Results" with structured tables, statuses, and "WELL verdicts." The tool may have technically processed values, but the inputs were model-inferred. Hermes laundered inference through WELL to create institutional authority.

### Causal overclaim
- "What killed him: The body he commodified" (Sandow) — cause of death disputed (brain hemorrhage vs. aortic aneurysm, no autopsy)
- "What killed him: The strength he mythologized" (Milo) — death is legend, not established history
- "What killed him: The mass he maintained for income" (Cyr) — kidney disease cause not isolatable to mass alone

### Factual errors
- Cyr died at 49, not 48
- "Almost nothing survived" for Cyr is false — records, monuments, thousands of performances remain in Canadian history
- Milo's age at death is unknown; his legendary death (torn apart by wolves) cannot be treated as historical fact

### Agency removal
All three men were presented as passive victims: "used by the market," "used by the city," "used by the family." Reality: Sandow actively built and controlled his commercial empire. Cyr organized shows, built enterprises, achieved financial success. They participated, then the participation became costly.

### Narrative symmetry bias
Three deaths, three clean morals, three poetic endings. The symmetry felt profound. That feeling was the warning signal.
