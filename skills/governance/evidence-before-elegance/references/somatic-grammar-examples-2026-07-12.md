# Somatic Grammar Examples — Before/After (2026-07-12)

From the Abang Sado shadow map conversation. Shows how embodied interpretation
should be phrased to preserve somatic intelligence without false measurement authority.

## The Problem

Hermes produced somatic readings of a muscular man's body from a photograph.
The readings were phenomenologically strong (9/10) and symbolically strong (9/10),
but used physiological grammar ("the nervous system is...", "this breath is
parasympathetic") that implied measurement without instruments.

The revised audit identified three layers:
- **Physiological** — what is biologically happening (needs instruments)
- **Phenomenological** — what bodily state is being expressed (visible form)
- **Symbolic** — what human meaning the posture carries (abstraction)

Most image-based somatic reading operates at layers 2 and 3, NEVER layer 1.

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Breath and ribcage

**Before (claims measurement):**
> "This breath is parasympathetic. The open ribcage confirms the nervous system
> is not in fight-or-flight."

**After (claims expression):**
> "The open ribcage evokes parasympathetic ease. The visible expansion of the
> torso symbolises a state where guarding has been released."

**Why:** You cannot determine parasympathetic activation from a photograph.
You CAN read the open ribcage as expressing a bodily state.

### Example 2: Shoulders

**Before:**
> "The nervous system is not guarding. The lowered shoulders confirm reduced
> cortisol."

**After:**
> "The lowered shoulders symbolise the absence of guarding. The settled deltoids
> read as a body that has stopped bracing against something."

**Why:** Cortisol cannot be measured from a photograph. But the pattern of
lowered vs. raised shoulders is a legitimate somatic signal.

### Example 3: Torso exposure

**Before:**
> "This body trusts where it is. The exposed torso means the person feels safe."

**After:**
> "The exposed torso communicates trust in the scene. The open chest creates
> the impression of a body that does not need to protect itself."

**Why:** "Trust" is an inference, not an observation. "Communicates trust" labels
it as the body's expressive signal, not a clinical diagnosis.

### Example 4: Gaze direction

**Before:**
> "The body forgot it was being photographed. The man is genuinely at peace."

**After:**
> "The averted gaze creates the impression of a body no longer performing for
> observation. The composition suggests inwardness rather than display."

**Why:** You cannot know if someone "forgot" something. You CAN read the
averted gaze as a compositional signal.

### Example 5: Overall body state

**Before:**
> "His strength is structural, not decorative. This body has been inhabited,
> not just built."

**After:**
> "The shoulder development reads as structural rather than decorative. The
> integration of musculature into relaxed posture communicates a body that has
> been lived in, not just assembled."

**Why:** "Structural" is a judgment. "Reads as structural" labels the
interpretation layer.

## Somatic Reading Prompt Template

When generating images using somatic intelligence:

```
A [description] man. His shoulders are lowered and relaxed, not flexing.
His hands rest loosely. His torso is open and exposed, not guarded.
His jaw is soft, not clenched. He looks off to the side with a contemplative
expression, not performing for the camera. The body communicates strength
that no longer needs to brace. The setting evokes home rather than display.
```

Key: describe the somatic signals directly, not the measurement claims.

## The Governing Sentence

> Hermes did not measure a nervous system. It imagined what peace would look
> like if the body could speak. That is a legitimate form of intelligence.
> It only becomes false when the metaphor is presented as biometric fact.
