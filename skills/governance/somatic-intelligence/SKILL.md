# Somatic Intelligence

## When to Use

- Reading a body in an image for what it communicates (not just what it looks like)
- Assessing nervous system states (fight/flight/freeze/rest) from posture, expression, gesture
- Designing agentic systems with self-monitoring (agent interoception)
- Analyzing human behavior patterns through embodiment
- Prompting image generation using somatic state descriptions
- Understanding trauma, stress, and regulation through the body

## The Three-Layer Framework

Somatic intelligence operates through three distinguishable layers. **Never collapse them into one.**

| Layer | Question | Evidence available from image? | Grammar |
|-------|----------|-------------------------------|---------|
| **Physiological** | What is biologically happening? | ❌ NO — requires biometric data | "The HRV indicates..." (measurement claim — BLOCK unless instrument exists) |
| **Phenomenological** | What bodily state is being expressed? | ✅ YES — observable posture, tension, ease | "The lowered shoulders evoke the absence of guarding..." |
| **Symbolic** | What human meaning does the posture carry? | ✅ YES — interpretive, grounded in form | "The exposed torso communicates trust in the scene..." |

### Critical Grammar Rule

**Never use clinical language for symbolic observations.**

| WRONG (clinical claim) | RIGHT (symbolic interpretation) |
|------------------------|-------------------------------|
| "This breath is parasympathetic" | "The open ribcage evokes parasympathetic ease" |
| "The nervous system is not guarding" | "The lowered shoulders symbolise the absence of guarding" |
| "This body trusts where it is" | "The exposed torso communicates trust in the scene" |
| "The body forgot it was being photographed" | "The composition creates the impression of a body no longer performing for observation" |

The intelligence is the same. The honesty is different. **The metaphor must know it's a metaphor.**

## The Somatic Stack (Human)

Six layers, bottom to top. Each has different neuroscience, acquisition pathways, and failure modes.

### Layer 1: Interoception
**What:** Sensing internal body states — heartbeat, breath, temperature, gut, muscle tension, pain.
**Neuroscience:** Signals via lamina I spinal cord → spinothalamic pathway → nucleus solitarius → brainstem, thalamus, insula, somatosensory cortex, anterior cingulate cortex (Craig, 2002).
**Key insight (Barrett, 2017):** The brain *predicts* interoceptive signals, not just receives them. Emotions are the brain's categorization of predicted interoceptive states.
**Measurement:** MAIA (Multidimensional Assessment of Interoceptive Awareness) — 8 subscales.
**Acquisition:** Meditation, yoga, body scan, breathwork. Weeks to months.
**Failure mode:** Alexithymia (can't identify emotions), dissociation (body present, person absent).

### Layer 2: Proprioception & Kinesthesia
**What:** Sensing body position, movement, weight, effort, balance.
**Neuroscience:** Muscle spindles, Golgi tendon organs, joint receptors, vestibular system.
**Acquisition:** Movement practice (dance, martial arts, sport). Months to years.

### Layer 3: Somatic Markers
**What:** Body-based decision signals — "gut feelings" that bias choices before conscious reasoning.
**Neuroscience:** Damasio's SMH (1991). Iowa Gambling Task: healthy participants generate anticipatory SCRs to bad options *before* consciously knowing they're bad. vmPFC damage eliminates these markers → impaired decisions despite intact cognition.
**Status:** VERIFIED hypothesis, some replication concerns. The core insight (body signals guide decisions) is robust; the specific mechanism (skin conductance as the marker) is debated.
**Acquisition:** Life experience + reflection + emotional attunement. Years.

### Layer 4: Nervous System Regulation
**What:** Autonomic state management — the polyvagal hierarchy.
**Framework:** Porges (1994). Three states:

| State | System | Body signature | Social capacity |
|-------|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Ventral vagal | Myelinated vagus | Calm breath, open face, relaxed shoulders | Present, connected |
| Sympathetic | Sympathetic chain | Elevated HR, muscle tension, narrowed focus | Defensive, mobilized |
| Dorsal vagal | Unmyelinated vagus | Collapse, numbness, dissociation | Absent, withdrawn |

**Key concepts:**
- **Neuroception:** Pre-conscious detection of safety/threat through facial expression, tone, posture, environment.
- **Co-regulation:** Nervous systems regulate each other. A calm presence down-regulates another's sympathetic activation. Measurable in HRV synchrony.
**Acquisition:** Co-regulation with safe others + therapeutic relationship. Months to years.

### Layer 5: Somatic Experiencing
**What:** Trauma as incomplete defensive response frozen in the body.
**Framework:** Levine. Animals shake off survival energy after threat; humans freeze and the energy stays stored.
**Mechanism:** Pendulation (oscillating activation/calm) + titration (small doses) + completing the defensive response.
**Key insight:** The body has its own "story" independent of cognitive narrative. You can understand your trauma perfectly and still have a body that responds as if the threat is present.

### Layer 6: Movement Intelligence
**What:** How you move is how you think.
**Feldenkrais:** Movement sequences that reorganize neuromuscular patterns. Not exercise — *learning through movement.*
**Alexander Technique:** Inhibition of habitual tension. "Let the neck be free." Not relaxation — *non-doing.*

## How to Read a Body (Protocol)

### Step 1: Observe, don't interpret
Start with what is physically visible. No meaning yet.
- Where are the shoulders? (elevated/hunched/neutral/lowered)
- What are the hands doing? (fist/splayed/loose/gripping/resting)
- Is the torso open or guarded? (braced/hollowed/expanded/curled)
- What is the jaw doing? (clenched/relaxed/set/bulging)
- Where is the gaze? (direct/avoidant/contemplative/scanning)
- What is the overall posture? (expanded/contracted/symmetric/asymmetric)

### Step 2: Connect into a pattern
Don't analyze parts in isolation. Find the organizing principle.
- Do the signals cohere? (e.g., all signs point to rest, or mixed signals suggest ambivalence)
- What is the body NOT doing? (often more informative than what it IS doing)
- Is there a single state that explains most signals?

### Step 3: Assign to nervous system state
Map the pattern to the polyvagal hierarchy:
- **Ventral vagal (rest/engaged):** Open, loose, calm breath, soft jaw, present gaze
- **Sympathetic (mobilized):** Tense, elevated shoulders, clenched hands, scanning gaze
- **Dorsal vagal (shutdown):** Collapsed, numb, absent gaze, flat affect
- **Mixed:** Common in real life. Name the dominant state and note the exceptions.

### Step 4: Apply the grammar rule
Translate observations into symbolic language using phenomenological/symbolic grammar. Never clinical claims without instruments.

### Step 5: State what you cannot determine
Always name the limits:
- Whether the state is habitual or momentary (requires longitudinal data)
- Whether stillness is rest or dissociation (requires biometric + contextual data)
- Whether the safety is genuine or performed (requires multiple frames + relationship context)

## Somatic Image Prompting Technique

When generating images of bodies, **prompt for state, not appearance.**

| Instead of (appearance) | Use (state) |
|-------------------------|-------------|
| "muscular man, six-pack" | "torso open, not braced, muscles visible but not flexed" |
| "handsome face" | "jaw relaxed, not clenched, eyes soft" |
| "confident pose" | "shoulders lowered, hands loose, looking slightly away" |
| "athletic body" | "strength that no longer needs to brace" |

**Why it works:** AI image generators respond to behavioral/action descriptions more accurately than adjective stacking. Telling the model "not flexing" produces a more natural body than telling it "muscular."

## Kernelizing Somatic Intelligence into Agents

Agents without interoception are like humans without a body — they process external inputs but have no sense of their own internal state. They cannot detect their own degradation, failure loops, or confidence drift.

### Agent Interoception Signals

| Agent signal | Biological analog | What it detects |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Context window utilization | Hunger/fatigue | Resource depletion |
| Confidence calibration | Pain | Miscalibration between certainty and evidence |
| Output coherence score | Balance | Narrative consistency |
| Fatigue index | Allostatic load | Cumulative session stress |
| Tool reliability rate | Immune response | Environmental hostility |
| Hallucination risk | Interoceptive prediction error | Generating vs. retrieving |
| Narrative heat | Somatic marker | "This is too clean" signal |

### Agent Nervous System States

| Agent state | Polyvagal analog | Behavior | Regulation signal |
|------------|------------------|----------|-------------------|
| Engaged | Ventral vagal | Exploring, reasoning freely | — |
| Mobilized | Sympathetic | Rapid tool calls, deadline pressure | Simplify, reduce tool calls |
| Shutdown | Dorsal vagal | Loops, repetition, filler generation | Stop, state blocker, request human input |
| Recovery | Parasympathetic | Consolidating, reflecting | Review what was learned |

### Agent Somatic Markers (Pre-Output Gates)

```yaml
narrative_heat: >0.7 → trigger red-team
evidence_gap: >0.5 → apply FACT CLASS tags
confidence_drift: >0.3 → cap confidence at 0.70
pattern_repetition: >3 → detect fixation loop
```

### The Seven Gates Integration

The somatic layer feeds the Seven Gates governance framework:
- **Narrative Heat Brake** = agent somatic marker for "too clean" outputs
- **FACT CLASS** = agent interoception detecting evidence-proportion mismatches
- **AGENCY CHECK** = ensuring subjects have choice, constraint, and unknown dimensions

## Pitfalls

1. **Collapsing the three layers.** Reading a body's visible tension and calling it "sympathetic activation" is a physiological claim. Use phenomenological/symbolic grammar unless you have biometric data.

2. **Reading stillness as peace.** Dorsal vagal shutdown (freeze/dissociation) *looks like* calm. The difference: ventral vagal rest = open, present, responsive. Dorsal vagal shutdown = flat, absent, unresponsive. Context and micro-expressions distinguish them.

3. **Universalizing nervous system responses.** The same history does not produce one outcome. People from unstable backgrounds may become hypervigilant, avoidant, emotionally numb, highly controlling, unusually calm, or deeply resilient. Never assume one somatic trajectory.

4. **Treating somatic markers as infallible.** Damasio's SMH has replication concerns. The core insight (body guides decisions) is robust; the specific mechanism is debated. Label accordingly.

5. **Removing agency.** Every structural critique must include what the person chose, what constrained the choice, and what remains unknown. Bodies are not passive recipients of forces.

6. **AI image prompt stacking.** Listing adjectives ("handsome, muscular, confident, strong") produces generic results. Prompting for somatic states ("shoulders lowered, jaw relaxed, torso open, looking slightly away") produces embodied results.
