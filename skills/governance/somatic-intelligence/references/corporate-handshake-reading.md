### Layer 3: Symbolic (what it communicates)

1. **The handshake angle.** Taufik's hand slightly on top — in folk reading, hosting/territorial authority. Descalzi's hand enters from above but lands under. Neither dominates. This is a peer handshake between equals, staged for camera.

2. **The yellow folder.** Taufik holds it alone, gripped against his own abdomen, angled inward. Not shared, not presented to Descalzi. This is a territorial marker — "this document is on my side." Descalzi's empty left hand signals he doesn't need to claim the paperwork. He already got what he came for.

3. **The smile asymmetry.** Taufik's Duchenne-genuine smile vs Descalzi's diplomatic-professional smile is the most significant signal in the frame. The Eni CEO has done this handshake a thousand times — his face knows how to perform warmth without investing it. Taufik's face broadcasts genuine pleasure. This asymmetry communicates: one party is more emotionally invested in this moment. In any negotiation, the party more visibly pleased at signing is rarely the party that got the better terms.

4. **The mutual lean.** Both bodies angle toward each other. Neither retreats. This communicates genuine rapport, not a hostile negotiation captured for PR. These men are comfortable with each other — or skilled enough to appear so.

5. **The staged nature.** The hands are clasped midair, separated from the table — this is a held-for-camera pose, not a candid greeting. Both men know exactly how this photo will be used. Their bodies are performing "partnership" with professional precision.

## Key Insight: The Emotional Investment Gap

The most actionable signal is the asymmetry of emotional investment. Descalzi's body says "another deal closed." Taufik's body says "I got what I wanted."

**Zygomatic asymmetry rule:** When one party in a signing photo shows a full Duchenne smile (orbicularis oculi + zygomatic major engaged) and the other shows a social/professional smile (zygomatic only, no eye involvement), the Duchenne party is likely the one who perceives themselves as having "won" the negotiation — or who has more to gain from the relationship going forward.

**Caveat:** Cultural factors (Malay corporate warmth norms vs Italian/European reserve) and personality factors (baseline expressiveness) can produce this asymmetry without signaling deal advantage. This reading is INT (interpreted), not CLAIM.

## How to Apply This Protocol

1. Observe the handshake: who's on top, grip type, distance, staging
2. Read the faces: Duchenne vs social smile, chin angle, eye engagement
3. Note what hands hold: documents, pens, nothing — and on whose side
4. Find the asymmetry: which body is more invested?
5. Check the staging: is this candid or held-for-camera?
6. State limits: cultural factors, personality baselines, habitual expressions
7. Never claim physiological states without instruments
