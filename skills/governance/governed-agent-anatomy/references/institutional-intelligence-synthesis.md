# Institutional Intelligence — Literature Synthesis

> For: arifOS Federation agentic institution design
> Forged: 2026-07-20 · Hermes + Arif deep research session
> Sources: Ostrom (Nobel 2009), Crossan/Lane/White (1999), March (1991), North (1990), Hila (2026), ContextGraph, Mnemoria, MultiA, PNAS (2025), Jarvenpaa & Välikangas (2025), Liu (2025), Sanchez Borboa

## Core Definition

**Institutional intelligence** = an institution's collective capacity to:
1. Perceive changes in its environment
2. Learn from experience (its own and others')
3. Integrate distributed knowledge across scales
4. Adapt before failure becomes irreversible
5. Preserve truthful memory across generational transitions

NOT the same as: having smart individuals, having good data/AI, or being efficient.

## Key Frameworks

### Ostrom's 8 Core Design Principles (CDPs)

Validated across thousands of communities worldwide. Institutions that survive centuries share these:

| # | Principle | Agentic Institution Translation |
|---|-----------|-------------------------------|
| 1 | Clearly defined boundaries | Who is in the institution? What is governed? |
| 2 | Proportional equivalence | Those who bear costs must share benefits |
| 3 | Collective-choice arrangements | Participants make/change the rules |
| 4 | Monitoring | Continuous, accountable to participants |
| 5 | Graduated sanctions | Light punishment first, escalate only if needed |
| 6 | Conflict-resolution mechanisms | Cheap, accessible, fast |
| 7 | Minimal recognition of rights | Local autonomy, external authorities don't override |
| 8 | Nested enterprises (polycentric) | Multiple layers, each autonomous but connected |

### Crossan's 4I Framework

Institutional learning flows: **Intuiting → Interpreting → Integrating → Institutionalizing**

Most institutions only do steps 3-4. They skip intuition and interpretation.

### March's Exploration-Exploitation Tradeoff

Intelligent institutions maintain BOTH: exploitation (refining known) AND exploration (searching new). The "competency trap" = getting so good at the old thing you can't learn the new.

### Hila's Reflective vs. Reliabilist Knowledge (2026)

- **Reflective** = you understand WHY something is true
- **Reliabilist** = you trust the source that says it's true
- LLMs produce reliabilist knowledge exclusively
- Collective justification: no single person holds the full justification for institutional knowledge

### Civilization Systems Model (Mnemoria)

Five nested levels of civilizational learning:
1. Individual Cognitive → seconds-years
2. Collective Intelligence → months-decades
3. Organizational Routines → years-centuries (survive turnover)
4. Institutional Codification → decades-centuries
5. Cultural Worldview → centuries-millennia

### Sanchez Borboa's Syntegrity Derivation

Derived Ostrom's 8 CDPs from first principles (ethical field theory). CDPs 1-3 = conditions for syntegrity's existence. CDPs 4-6 = stability. CDPs 7-8 = scaling. The derivation is to Ostrom what Newton was to Kepler.

## Three Failure Modes (Jarvenpaa & Välikangas 2025)

1. **Artifact-centricity** — knowledge encapsulated in digital artifacts, no social practice
2. **Acceleration** — speed replaces wisdom, react but don't learn
3. **Unvetted seeking-and-helping** — everyone asks AI, nobody verifies

## Civilizational Decline Mechanism (Liu 2025)

> "Institutional standards are not exogenous constants but the institutionalized expression of collective cognitive states. Individual cognitive drift, at sufficient scale, directly constitutes institutional drift."

Three channels: heterogeneity sealing, institutional migration of meaning supply, generational metacognitive rupture.
