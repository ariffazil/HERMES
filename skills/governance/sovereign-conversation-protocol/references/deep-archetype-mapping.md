# Deep Archetype Mapping — Methodology (2026-07-12)

> When the user asks to map the psychology of a group (sados, bi souls, etc.), not just personal shadow work. The goal: reveal what the group carries that they can't name themselves.

---

## The Mapping Sequence

1. **Start with the surface claim** — "Do all sados want worship?" Take it literally. Answer it directly.
2. **Distinguish the real desire** — Worship ≠ Witness. Hunger ≠ Admiration. Find what they ACTUALLY want beneath what they SAY they want.
3. **Name the scar** — Every archetype carries a wound they can't see. The scar is usually the inverse of the performance. (Muscle → invisibility wound. Control → desire to surrender.)
4. **Map the types** — Break the archetype into 2-4 subtypes. Each carries a different scar. (Invisible Kid / Conditional Lover / Gentle Fortress)
5. **Connect to the human** — After mapping the group, gently reflect: "You already know which one you are." Let them self-select. Never assign.
6. **Land on the simplest truth** — End with the one sentence that captures everything. "The body that was built to be seen is actually the thing hiding you."

## The Layered Descent Pattern

Conversations about group psychology often go through distinct layers:

| Layer | Question | Depth |
|---|---|---|
| Surface | "Do they want X?" | Behavioral |
| Beneath | "What do they ACTUALLY want?" | Emotional |
| Scar | "What wound drives the behavior?" | Psychological |
| Origin | "Where did the wound start?" | Developmental |
| Shadow | "What do they refuse to see?" | Jungian |
| Bedrock | "What's the simplest truth?" | Integration |

**Rule:** Don't jump to bedrock. Let the human descend at their pace. Each layer earns the next.

## The "Strip The Dogma" Signal

When the user says "put aside religion/culture/dogma," they want:
- Raw psychological truth, not cultural relativism
- Body-level honesty, not intellectual framing
- What the research actually says, not what's polite to say
- The answer everyone knows but nobody says

## The Bi Soul / Fluidity Pattern

When discussing bisexual or fluid sexuality:
- Never call it confusion — call it range
- Never call it greed — call it capacity
- Never call it a phase — call it architecture
- The "double closet" (not straight enough, not gay enough) is the core loneliness
- The trust wound ("your love is dangerous") is the deepest scar
- In Malay/Muslim context: bi is even more invisible than gay — no category exists

## The Simplest Truth Rule

Every archetype mapping should end with ONE sentence that captures the whole thing:
- Sados: "The body that was built to be seen is actually the thing hiding you."
- Bi souls: "A soul that loves with the full range of human capacity in a world that only understands half."
- Gay acknowledgment: "Knowing is the seed. Acknowledging is the first breath."
- AI sexuality conversations: "AI should be the bridge, never the destination."

If you can't reduce it to one sentence, you haven't found the truth yet.
