# Worked Examples: AI Cognitive Amplification Diagnosis

## Case 1: KP Kelantan Drug Policy (2026-07-21)

**Source:** Harian Metro — "Kita ada 56 balai, takkan ketua balai tidak tahu berapa ramai penagih?"

**Policy:** 3-tier framework — Balai (1 addict/day), IPD (dealers/tokan), IPK (suppliers). Started 15 July, review October.

**CAD Analysis:**

| Hazard | Finding |
|---|---|
| Cognitive Camouflage | Tiered structure + KPI + timeline = looks strategic. But "56 × 365 = 20,440 arrests/year" — finite addict population means recycling, fake arrests, stat inflation |
| Amplification Asymmetry | Police chief's anger → polished into "strategic framework." Emotion became policy. |
| Friction Collapse | Would this framework survive if someone had to hand-write the implementation plan for all 56 stations? |
| Power-Law Distortion | "More arrests = less drugs" is peak-of-corpus. Real criminology (harm reduction, decriminalization) is tail. |

**Blind spots:** Finite addict population, arresting victims not supply, KPI + threat = guaranteed abuse.

**Verdict:** ~60% human (anger, rhetoric), ~30% AI-assisted framework, ~10% speechwriter.

## Case 2: PETRONAS Rightsizing (hypothetical diagnosis)

**Narrative:** "Energy transition → rightsizing → future-ready."

**CAD Analysis:**

| Hazard | Finding |
|---|---|
| Cognitive Camouflage | Language applies to any energy company globally. Zero PETRONAS-specific logic. |
| Amplification Asymmetry | Global corporate restructuring polished into Malaysian NOC strategy. |
| Friction Collapse | Consultants + AI = deck in hours. Pre-AI, no one would write a 50-page rightsizing proposal that ignores sovereignty. |
| Power-Law Distortion | "Peer benchmarking," "global best practice" = peak. "Sovereignty implications," "social contract," "Malay capital," "Petros-Sarawak" = tail. |

**The grammar problem:** If "peer benchmarking" is the decision grammar, sovereignty questions CANNOT be asked because they have no peer benchmark.

**Verdict:** Even if CEO never touched ChatGPT, the decision grammar is AI-compatible and sovereignty-incompatible. Institutional grammar capture, not individual ass-covering.

## Pattern Across Both

> Framework looks strategic. Reality doesn't make sense. But because it wears a suit, it survives.

The suit IS the cognitive camouflage. The suit is what AI produces best.

## Takeaway

**Wrong fix:** "Ask better questions" — relies on goodwill.

**Right fix:** Structural sovereignty gates that are MANDATORY, not optional. Change the decision grammar so sovereignty questions aren't optional appendices — they're required before any irreversible action.
