# AI Cognitive Amplification Diagnosis

## Trigger

Arif sends a link to a policy article, corporate announcement, government strategy, or institutional output — OR asks "do you think they used AI for this?" — OR wants a structural diagnosis of why something feels "polished but stupid."

## Core Framework: The Four Amplification Hazards

### 1. Cognitive Camouflage
AI polishes stupidity into something that looks like policy.
- Coherence without grounding
- Syntactic rationality without causal validity
- Form independent of truth
- "Shiny turd" phenomenon

**Detection signal:** Framework language that could apply to ANY organization. Nothing domain-specific. Nothing that would fail if transplanted to a different country/industry.

### 2. Amplification Asymmetry
AI amplifies pattern, not quality.
- Idea bijak → jadi lebih bijak
- Idea bodoh → jadi lebih licin, lebih tersusun, lebih meyakinkan
- No truth gradient between the two outputs

**Detection signal:** The output LOOKS better than the input deserves. If the underlying idea is stupid but the presentation is professional, asymmetry is at work.

### 3. Friction Collapse
Pre-AI, stupid ideas died because nobody wanted to write 20 pages.
Post-AI, 5 seconds → 20 pages → bad ideas survive selection pressure.

**Detection signal:** Volume of output disproportionate to apparent effort. KPI frameworks, Gantt charts, risk matrices for things that don't warrant them.

### 4. Power-Law Distortion
AI discriminates on POPULARITY, not VALIDITY.
Training data is power-law distributed:
- Peak = what humans write most (bureaucratic, superficial, safe, median)
- Tail = what is true but niche (domain-specific, context-heavy, causally grounded)

AI amplifies the head, ignores the tail.

**Detection signal:** The argument sounds like "what everyone else is doing/saying." Benchmark-driven, peer-aligned, global-best-practice language. Nothing that requires local knowledge.

## Diagnostic Questions

When analyzing a policy/narrative, ask:

### Surface layer (what AI CAN do)
- [ ] Is the framework perfectly tiered/structured? (3 pillars, 5 levers, 7 strategic priorities)
- [ ] Are the KPIs clean and symmetrical?
- [ ] Does the language sound like it could apply to any organization in this sector globally?
- [ ] Are there Gantt charts, risk matrices, executive summaries for things that don't warrant them?

### Blind spot layer (what AI CANNOT do)
- [ ] Apa implikasi sovereignty?
- [ ] Apa kesan pada kontrak sosial?
- [ ] Apa kesan pada bargaining power jangka panjang?
- [ ] Siapa yang UNTUNG jika ini dilaksanakan? Siapa yang RUGI?
- [ ] Apa knowledge/keupayaan yang akan hilang kekal?
- [ ] Apa realiti spesifik-domain yang diabaikan?

If the surface layer is polished but the blind spot layer is absent → **AI-mediated reasoning confirmed at structural level.**

### Institutional grammar layer
- [ ] Adakah decision grammar membenarkan soalan-soalan blind spot ditanya?
- [ ] Atau adakah grammar itu sendiri menapisnya?
- [ ] Adakah "peer benchmarking" dan "global best practice" telah menggantikan analisis spesifik-konteks?

## Structural Fix Pattern

**Wrong fix:** "Ask better questions" — relies on goodwill of decision-maker.

**Right fix:** Structural sovereignty gates that are MANDATORY, not OPTIONAL.

Example for PETRONAS-level decisions:
1. Sovereignty impact assessment (independent, not consultant-produced)
2. 10-year capability projection
3. Social contract impact analysis
4. Federal-state/federation implication analysis

These gates must be REQUIRED by the decision grammar, not offered as optional appendices.
