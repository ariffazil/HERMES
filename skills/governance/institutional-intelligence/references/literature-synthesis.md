# Literature Synthesis: Institutional Intelligence

> Condensed from 8+ peer-reviewed papers and canonical theorists.
> Session: 2026-07-20. Sources: Ostrom, North, Acemoglu/Robinson, Argyris/Schön, Heylighen, Hila, Liu, Jarvenpaa/Välikangas, Crossan/Lane/White.

---

## North (1990, Nobel 1993): Rules of the Game

- **Institutions ≠ organizations.** Institutions are the rules constraining/enabling organizations.
- Institutions = formal constraints (laws, constitutions) + informal constraints (norms, taboos, customs).
- **Adaptive efficiency** = can rules evolve with environment? Rigid institutions die.
- **Credible commitment** = do rules bind the powerful, not just the weak?
- **Path-dependent memory** = institutions carry their history. Can't understand them without their formation story.
- Dark finding: "Institutions are not necessarily created to be socially efficient; they serve those with bargaining power."

## Ostrom (1990, Nobel 2009): Polycentric Governance

- **Polycentricity** = many centers of decision-making, formally independent, functionally interdependent.
- 8 Design Principles for long-surviving commons institutions:
  1. Clearly defined boundaries
  2. Proportional equivalence (benefits match costs)
  3. Collective-choice arrangements (participants modify rules)
  4. Monitoring (accountable to participants, not outsiders)
  5. Graduated sanctions (light first, escalate only if needed)
  6. Conflict-resolution mechanisms (cheap, accessible, local)
  7. Minimal recognition of rights (external authorities don't override)
  8. Nested enterprises (polycentric layers)
- Key finding: user-managed systems often outperform government-managed. Communication dramatically increases cooperation.
- Trust is the central variable in cooperation — but trust comes from *structure*, not personality.
- "One size fits all" kills institutions.

## Acemoglu & Robinson (2012): Extractive vs. Inclusive

| Extractive | Inclusive |
|-----------|-----------|
| Extract wealth: many → few | Broad participation in economic/political life |
| Protect elite power, suppress creative destruction | Enable creative destruction |
| Political power → economic extraction → more political power | Broad political power → broad economic opportunity |
| Stable collapse (look stable until they aren't) | Creative stability (adapt through renewal) |

- Extraction peaks. Inclusion compounds over civilizational time.
- Institutions fail when they choose extraction over inclusion.
- Geography/culture/ignorance do NOT explain national success — institutions do.

## Argyris & Schön (1974): Double-Loop Learning

- **Single-loop (SLL):** fix the error, don't question the system. "Thermostat too cold → turn up heat."
- **Double-loop (DLL):** examine and change governing variables. "Why is the thermostat set this way?"
- DLL is documented in <5% of observed organizational improvement behaviors.
- Barriers: defensive routines, unawareness (espoused theory ≠ theory-in-use), leadership gaps.
- **Espoused theory:** what people SAY they follow. **Theory-in-use:** what they ACTUALLY follow.
- Model I (unilateral control, winning, suppressing negative feelings) → single-loop only.
- Model II (valid information, free choice, personal responsibility) → enables double-loop.

## Heylighen (2007): Global Superorganism

- Society = multicellular organism. Individuals = cells. Communication = nervous system.
- **Global Brain** = internet + AI + collective cognition = emergent planetary intelligence.
- Key mechanisms:
  - **Stigmergy:** agents leave traces (wiki edits, code commits) that guide other agents without direct communication.
  - **Ephemeralization:** doing more with less (Buckminster Fuller).
  - **Self-organization:** order from local interactions, not central planning.
  - **Challenge propagation:** unsolved problems bubble up to higher organizational levels.
- Civilizational intelligence = capacity to sense threats at any scale, propagate challenges, learn via stigmergic traces, adapt faster than environment degrades.

## Hila (2026): Reflective vs. Reliabilist Knowledge

- **Reliabilist:** "I trust the source that says X is true." LLMs produce this.
- **Reflective:** "I understand WHY X is true and can justify it." Humans (who did the work) have this.
- **Collective justification:** no single person holds full justification for most institutional knowledge. Intelligence is in the CHAIN.
- **The scaling threat:** when institutions outsource reflective knowledge to AI-only outputs, the chain of justification hollows out. Surface looks smart. Reality: nobody understands WHY.

## Liu (2025): Civilizational Cognitive Drift

- Three channels through which individual cognitive decline → civilizational epistemic crisis:
  1. **Heterogeneity sealing:** personalized confirmation loops collapse interpretive diversity.
  2. **Institutional migration of meaning supply:** authority to define truth moves from institutions to privatized AI.
  3. **Generational metacognitive rupture:** LLMs shorten uncertainty exposure → generation that never learns to think about thinking → can't transmit what it lacks.
- Key quote: "Institutional standards are not exogenous constants but the institutionalized expression of collective cognitive states. Individual cognitive drift, at sufficient scale, directly constitutes institutional drift."
- **Drift is invisible. Extraction is visible.** Both kill institutions.

## Crossan, Lane & White (1999): The 4I Framework

| Process | Level | What Happens |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Intuiting | Individual | Pattern recognition, pre-conscious insight |
| Interpreting | Group | Naming, explaining, sharing the insight |
| Integrating | Group→Organization | Coordinated action, changed practices |
| Institutionalizing | Organization | Embedding into systems, rules, routines |

- Trap: most institutions only do steps 3-4 (integrate, institutionalize). Skip 1-2.
- Result: bureaucracies full of rules nobody remembers the reason for.

## Jarvenpaa & Välikangas (2025): Technology-Enabled Intelligence Loss

- Three trajectories:
  1. **Artifact-centricity:** knowledge consumed as digital artifacts, not practiced.
  2. **Acceleration:** adaptation too fast to draw on existing knowledge. Speed replaces wisdom.
  3. **Unvetted seeking-and-helping:** information flows without truth/relevance/currency assessment.
- Brutal finding: intelligent technology can make organizations LESS intelligent.

## March (1991): Exploration-Exploitation Tradeoff

- **Exploitation** = refining what you know (efficient, predictable, short-term returns).
- **Exploration** = searching for new possibilities (risky, uncertain, long-term returns).
- **Competency trap:** getting so good at the old thing you can't learn the new thing.
- Intelligent institutions maintain both simultaneously.

---

## Synthesis: The Convergent Definition

**Institutional intelligence = capacity to (1) sense environmental change, (2) modify governing rules in response, (3) preserve accumulated wisdom across time, (4) prevent extraction by any participant, and (5) maintain sovereign accountability — without depending on the virtue of individual participants.**
