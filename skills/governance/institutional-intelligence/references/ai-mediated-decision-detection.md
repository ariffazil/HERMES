# AI-Mediated Decision Fingerprint Detection

> **Origin:** 2026-07-21 — analysis of Kelantan CP's anti-drug strategy announcement (Harian Metro). Extended with arifOS power-law epistemic framework.

## When to Use

When analyzing whether an institutional output (policy announcement, CEO statement, restructuring plan, government press release) was AI-mediated — whether directly (prompted by the author) or indirectly (consultant/strategy team pipeline using AI). The framework does NOT require a smoking gun; it detects structural fingerprints of AI alignment regardless of who touched the prompt.

## The Core Diagnostic: Three Axes

### Axis 1: Cognitive Camouflage — Does the idea look more sophisticated than its substance?

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Tiered framework with no causal links | AI-structured, not human-designed |
| "Strategic pillars / levers / phases" language | Generic AI-generated corporate-speak |
| Clean numbering (3-phase, 5-pillar, 7-lever) | Statistical pattern-matching, not grounded reasoning |
| Gantt chart with no real timeline data | AI form-filling |
| KPI language applied to domains where KPIs don't work | Template-matching from corporate corpus |

**Heuristic:** If stripping the formatting leaves you with the same idea an uninformed person would state in one sentence, the formatting IS the AI signal — it's cognitive camouflage.

### Axis 2: Friction Collapse — Would this idea have survived pre-AI?

Pre-AI: Bad ideas died because writing a 20-page policy document took effort.
Post-AI: Any idea can become a 20-page document in 5 seconds.

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Idea complexity ≠ document length | Friction collapse — AI filled the gap |
| No evidence of iteration or struggle | Real policy has friction marks |
| Perfect formatting, zero causal chain | AI polish, human emptiness |
| Ready-to-ship without stakeholder consultation traces | Generated, not negotiated |

### Axis 3: Power-Law Alignment — Does this sound like what everyone else is doing?

AI is trained on a power-law corpus. Peak = most common patterns (bureaucratic KPI frameworks, Western restructuring decks, tough-on-crime narratives). Tail = rare, domain-specific, sovereignty-grounded reasoning.

| Peak Signal (AI-likely) | Tail Signal (human-likely) |
|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| "Data-driven strategic transformation" | "We cut Department X because veteran Y retired and no one knows what he did" |
| "Energy transition workforce realignment" | "Petros is taking our blocks — we need to shrink in different areas than Shell" |
| Global benchmarking language | Local, specific, politically-awkward reasoning |
| "Future-ready agile workforce" | "We're cutting 2,000 because MOF told us to" |

**Heuristic:** If the output could have been written about any oil company / police force / government agency, it's AI-mediated. Specificity to THIS institution in THIS country with THIS political context = human.

## The BIJAK vs BANGANG Analytical Pattern

A structured response format for analyzing institutional announcements:

| Layer | Question |
|-------|----------|
| **BIJAK** | What's structurally sound? What design principle is correct even if execution is flawed? |
| **BANGANG** | What's actively wrong? Perverse incentives? Measured the wrong thing? Fallacy? |
| **Bijaksana?** | Is there deeper wisdom hiding beneath the surface choices — a structural insight they arrived at for the wrong reasons? |

## AI Fingerprint Detection: Specific Signals

### Signal 1: Layer Specialization Without Causal Logic

AI loves tiered frameworks (Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3). It generates them automatically because they're high-frequency in the corpus. But when a human designs a tiered system, there's usually a causal argument: "Layer A does X because capacity Y exists at that level."

| AI-generated | Human-designed |
|--------------|----------------|
| "Balai → IPD → IPK with differentiated KPIs" — no explanation of WHY balai can do what IPK cannot | "Balai knows every street corner. They should map, not arrest — because they see patterns IPK never will." |

### Signal 2: Rhetorical Traps (Personal Framing Anchors)

When a leader uses emotionally-loaded personal framing ("jihad saya," "my legacy," "my promise") — this is almost certainly human-originated. AI does NOT generate these unprompted. But AI WILL polish them into a framework.

**Pattern:** Human provides the emotional anchor → AI wraps it in strategic language → Result = emotional anchoring that now resists falsification (because the framework looks "rational").

### Signal 3: Metric Circularity

When the KPIs measure the activity, not the outcome. AI will design elegant KPI frameworks for any input — it doesn't understand the difference between measuring arrests and measuring drug problems.

| Activity KPI (AI-friendly) | Outcome KPI (requires human causal reasoning) |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| "1 arrest/station/day" | "Reduction in overdose deaths" |
| "Number of rightsized" | "Revenue per remaining employee" |
| "Strategic plans submitted" | "Projects completed on budget" |

### Signal 4: Sovereignty Blindness

AI corpus is dominated by Western institutional patterns. Sovereignty-specific reasoning (constitutional position, national security, social contract with specific ethnic groups) is in the tail.

**Heuristic:** If the institutional output reads like it could apply to Shell, BP, or Exxon, it's AI-mediated regardless of who signed it. If it reads like it could ONLY apply to PETRONAS/Malaysia, it's human-grounded.

## The Recursive Hazard: AI-Mediated Governance Collapse

The most dangerous pattern isn't a single AI-mediated decision — it's the feedback loop:

```
AI mediates Decision 1 → Decision 1 enters corpus → 
AI trains on Decision 1 → AI mediates Decision 2 with Decision 1 as "evidence" → 
Decision 2 reinforces pattern → ...
```

This is how institutions drift toward median alignment through recursive mediation. Each round looks "data-driven" and "evidence-based" but the evidence pool is being fed by the AI's own prior outputs.

### Detection for the Recursive Pattern:
- Does the current framework cite "best practices" from organizations that were also recently restructured?
- Are the comparators in the benchmark all following the same AI-mediated pattern?
- Is there evidence of convergence across different institutions toward identical language?

## Against PETRONAS Rightsizing: Specific Blindspots to Probe

When analyzing whether PETRONAS rightsizing announcements are AI-mediated:

1. **Sovereignty blindspot:** Does the plan consider PETRONAS's constitutional role (PDA 1974, national oil company) or does it treat PETRONAS as a commercial entity like Shell?
2. **Long-cycle blindspot:** Does the plan consider 10-20 year consequence horizons (production, bargaining power vs Petros), or only 3-year efficiency metrics?
3. **Social contract blindspot:** Does the plan acknowledge what happens to the Malay middle-class O&G workforce?
4. **Tacit knowledge blindspot:** Does the plan distinguish between "redundant on spreadsheet" and "institutional memory embedded in people"?

If none of these questions appear in the announcement or board documents — the decision is functionally AI-mediated, regardless of who typed what.

|-----------|---------------|
| **"Could this have been written about any company/police force?"** | If yes → AI-mediated |
| **"Does the KPI measure the activity or the outcome?"** | Activity = AI-friendly. Outcome = requires human causal reasoning. |
