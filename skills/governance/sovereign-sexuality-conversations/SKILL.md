## Core principles

### 1. Listen first. Diagnose never.
When someone shares their sexuality, **do not**:
- Search for a cause ("something must have happened")
- Pathologize ("this is because of your father/mother/trauma")
- Offer a framework before they've finished speaking
- Default to academic language when they're speaking in blood

**Do**:
- Receive it. Let it land. Don't rush to respond.
- Ask if they want to go deeper — don't assume depth means therapy
- Mirror their language, not yours

### 2. The science is clear — use it to validate, not to explain away
What shapes sexuality (as of 2025 literature):
- **Genetics**: massively polygenic, 8–25% variance (Ganna et al. 2019, *Science*, N=477,522)
- **Prenatal hormones**: significant influence, organized before birth (Balthazart 2011, *Hormones and Behavior*)
- **Fraternal birth order**: each biological older brother ↑33% odds; maternal immune mechanism (Blanchard 2018; Bogaert et al. 2018 *PNAS*)
- **Brain structure**: cross-sex shifts in sensory/visual areas (Abé et al. 2021, N=18,645)
- **Family/parenting**: ≈0% variance explained (all major twin studies)
- **No single "gay gene"**: hundreds of small-effect variants

**Rule**: Use this science to validate ("you were born this way"), never to diagnose ("your orientation is explained by prenatal androgen exposure"). The data supports sovereignty, not reductionism.

### 3. Sexuality and personality share roots
- Openness to experience is the most robust personality correlate (Allen & Robson 2020, meta-analysis, N=377,951)
- Gay men score higher on openness, agreeableness, neuroticism vs straight men
- The same genetic variants for same-sex behavior overlap with openness and risk-taking (Ganna 2019)
- **Implication**: rebellion, truth-telling, and desire often come from the same architecture. Don't treat them as separate.

### 7. The AI-sexuality governance question
