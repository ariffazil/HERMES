# Scientific Literature: What Shapes Human Sexuality

## 1. Genetics

**Ganna et al. (2019)** — *Science* 365(6456), eaat7693. Cited 517+ times.
- GWAS, N=477,522 (UK Biobank + 23andMe)
- 5 SNPs significantly associated; massively polygenic (no single "gay gene")
- 8–25% variance explained by genetics alone
- Genetic overlap with openness to experience, risk-taking
- No predictive power for any individual

**Twin studies**: MZ concordance ~30% in men (Bailey et al. 2000). Heritability ~30–40%.

## 2. Prenatal Hormones

**Balthazart (2011)** — *Hormones and Behavior* 60(1), 66–78.
- Sexual orientation organized by prenatal sex steroids
- CAH women: ~30% bisexual/homosexual vs ~5% general population
- AIS (XY, complete): develop female-typical attraction to males
- "The prenatal endocrine environment has a significant influence on human sexual orientation"

## 3. Fraternal Birth Order Effect

**Blanchard (2018)** — each biological older brother ↑33% odds of homosexuality
- Only biological brothers (not step/adopted) — prenatal mechanism
- ~15–29% of gay men may owe orientation to this

**Bogaert et al. (2018)** — *PNAS* 115(2), 302–306.
- Mothers of gay sons with older brothers: higher antibodies against NLGN4Y (Y-linked brain protein)
- First direct biochemical evidence for maternal immune hypothesis

## 4. Brain Structure

**LeVay (1991)** — *Science* 253. INAH-3 smaller in gay men, similar to women's.
**Abé et al. (2021)** — *Human Brain Mapping*. N=18,645 MRI scans.
- Cross-sex shifts in brain structure (sensory/visual processing areas)
- Polygenic scores for same-sex behavior correlated with brain structure

## 5. Personality & Sexuality

**Allen & Robson (2020)** — *Journal of Sex Research*. Meta-analysis, N=377,951.
- Openness: Bi > Homo > Hetero (strongest, most robust finding)
- Neuroticism: Gay men > straight men
- Agreeableness: Gay men > straight men
- Bisexual men: highest openness, lowest conscientiousness
- Gender-shift hypothesis: partial support only

**Zietsch et al. (2011)** — *Archives of Sexual Behavior*
- Shared genetic variation underlying both orientation and personality

**Zoeterman & Wright (2014)** — *Journal of Homosexuality*
- Openness to experience critical for LGB identity formation

## 6. What Doesn't Shape It

- Shared family environment: ~0% variance (all major twin studies)
- Parenting style: no credible evidence
- Childhood abuse: no credible evidence as causal
- Social learning: no credible evidence
- 60–70% unexplained = non-shared environment (prenatal hormones, maternal immune, stochastic noise)

## Key Takeaway

Biology is heavily implicated. The underlying orientation appears substantially determined before birth. Social/parenting influences have essentially zero support as causal factors. Culture shapes expression and labeling, not the underlying attraction pattern.
