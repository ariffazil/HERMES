# Briefing Template

Use this structure by default. Omit empty sections rather than filling them
with weak material. Telegram-optimized (~4000 char).

# ASI World Sensorium

`[date] · cut-off [time and timezone] · comparison window [start–end]`

## Dominant Change

State the one development that most changes the prior world model. Label each
sentence where the status changes. 2-3 sentences max.

## War, Geopolitics and Energy Arteries

Report material changes, affected chokepoints, escalation or de-escalation
indicators, and unresolved claims.

## Malaysia

Cover only consequential economic, political, institutional, energy, trade,
currency, subsidy, and household signals.

## GEOX and Energy

Cover oil, gas, LNG, shipping, physical assets, basin or field developments,
and geological constraints. Every price: `value · instrument · benchmark/contract · currency · timestamp · source`.

## WEALTH and Markets

Present current benchmarked data, transmission mechanisms, downside, and
reversibility. Do not issue trading instructions.

## AI and Agentic Systems

Include only developments that change capability, cost, access, governance,
interoperability, or deployment reality.

## Meaning for Arif

1. What changed.
2. What did not change.
3. What deserves attention now.

## Watch Horizon

### Next 24 hours

List observable triggers, not predictions presented as facts.

### Next 7 days

List scheduled decisions, data releases, meetings, or escalation points.

### Structural signal

Identify the slow-moving institutional or system change.

## Evidence Ledger

| Claim | Label | Evidence basis | Timestamp | Hold |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [claim] | OBS/DER/INT/SPEC/UNK | [primary or corroborating basis] | [event/data time] | none/[gap] |

Include only the most load-bearing claims.

## Release Status

`RELEASE`, `RELEASE_WITH_HOLDS`, or `HOLD`

State the named unresolved items. Do not attach an arbitrary numeric score.
