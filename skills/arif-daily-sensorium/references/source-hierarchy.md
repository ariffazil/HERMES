# Source Hierarchy

Use the strongest available evidence for each claim. Source prestige does not
replace direct relevance.

## Tier 1 — Primary Authority

- official government, election commission (SPR), central bank (BNM), regulator, court, military, treaty, company filing, exchange, statistical agency (DOSM), or vendor documentation;
- direct transcript, legal text, policy document, filing, dataset, model card, release note, or official price feed;
- direct statement by an identified responsible institution.

Use Tier 1 for elections, dissolutions, laws, appointments, monetary policy,
official statistics, model names, API prices, and product availability.

## Tier 2 — High-Quality Independent Reporting

Major wire services and established international or national newsrooms with
named reporting. Prefer reports that link to documents, quote responsible
officials, and distinguish allegation from confirmation.

Use at least two independent Tier 2 sources when no primary source is
accessible and the claim is consequential or surprising.

## Tier 3 — Specialist Reporting

Reputable energy, shipping, finance, technology, defence, and geoscience
publications for domain detail. Check whether the article is reporting
original evidence or repeating another outlet.

## Tier 4 — Discovery-Only Sources

Search snippets, aggregators, Wikipedia, social media, video titles,
newsletters, forums, and reposts are LEADS. They may reveal a query term
or source name, but cannot alone establish a load-bearing fact.

## Independence Test

Two sources are NOT independent when both repeat the same wire report,
press release, unnamed account, or social post. Trace the provenance
before counting corroboration.

**Session scar (2026-07-16):** 10 outlets citing Reuters = 1 source, not 10.

## Local-Language Rule

For Malaysian politics and policy, search English AND Bahasa Melayu.
Prefer SPR, gazettes, official state notices, Bernama, and established
Malaysian reporting before foreign summaries.

## Conflict Rule

When reliable sources conflict:

1. State the conflict.
2. Distinguish what each source actually claims.
3. Prefer the source closest to the event or authoritative record.
4. Assign `UNK` to the unresolved part.
5. Do not hide disagreement inside a smooth narrative.
